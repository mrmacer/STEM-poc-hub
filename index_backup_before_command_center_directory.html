THE ORIGIN STORY

The PA STEM PoC Network didn't start as a formal structure — it grew from a shared problem: IUs across the commonwealth were doing great STEM work in isolation. The PAsmart grant ecosystem created the infrastructure to change that. Today, this network is one of the most active cross-IU collaboration spaces in Pennsylvania education.

Your role as a PoC (Point of Contact) is to be the STEM connective tissue for your IU — linking PDE priorities, statewide resources, and your local districts into something coherent and useful.

The Org Triangle

PAIMS (ed tech + instructional media) · PAIUCC (curriculum coordinators) · STEM PoC (you) — these three groups share an annual joint meeting every March and increasingly overlap in mission.

FOUNDATION

PAsmart Launches STEMinPA

Federal grant funding creates the framework for statewide IU collaboration around STEM, CS, and student-centered learning.

GROWTH

Workgroups Proliferate

Science, T&E, Environmental Literacy, CS, Student Competitions, Equity/TaC/Inclusion — the network self-organizes around real practitioner needs.

NOW

Full Ecosystem Integration

STEM PoCs are embedded in ORBIT data reporting, WestEd CoPs, STEELS rollout, and the emerging AI/CS frontier. You're joining at a pivotal moment.

SOW — DELIVERABLE MAP

The SoW Menu helps PoCs connect local STEM work to statewide STEMinPA priorities. Think of it as the bridge between what you are already doing, what the grant expects, and what eventually needs to be documented in ORBIT. Turn the SoW from compliance into strategy.

// The SOW Is Designed to Overlap

The interconnected and overlapping nature of STEM work means activities often align with multiple deliverables — this is by design. The goal is not to silo work into isolated buckets, but to build coherent systems that connect STEM, CS, workforce, student-centered learning, inclusion, and ecosystem development.

The focus should remain on impact, collaboration, and evidence — not strict accounting of days.
// What Actually Counts as a STEM Day? ▾
  • A day = minimum 7 hours of STEM-related work
  • Hours can be consecutive or non-consecutive across a calendar day
  • Activities may align to multiple deliverables simultaneously — log them all
  • Cross-collaborative work with partners, TaC, or other PoCs counts
  • IU leaders have flexibility to apply days based on local context and priorities

Examples of qualifying work:

  • Planning a regional STEM ecosystem convening
  • Designing AI professional learning tied to STEELS
  • Running a student competition planning meeting
  • Building statewide workgroup resources
  • Supporting district strategic planning for STEM
  • Collaborating with TaC on inclusive STEM access
Impact matters more than perfect categorization.

ORBIT REPORTING COMPANION

ORBIT is where STEMinPA work becomes documented evidence. Even if the tool changes next year, the thinking stays the same: name the work, connect it to a deliverable, explain the impact, and attach the proof. Move from activity tracking to impact storytelling.

A — ORBIT DECODER

Plain-language translation of the six things ORBIT asks you for in every entry.

Field 1
Activity / Event
What actually happened? Name the work — a workshop, a meeting, a convening, a resource you built.
Field 2
Deliverable Alignment
Which SoW bucket does this support? See the SoW Menu above — pick A through G.
Field 3
Description / Narrative
Why did this work matter? Two to four sentences connecting what happened to the bigger goal.
Field 4
Participants / Audience
Who was served or involved? Educators, administrators, students, community partners — be specific about numbers when possible.
Field 5
Evidence / Artifact
What proves the work happened? Agendas, sign-in sheets, resources, photos, links — attach or reference everything.
Field 6
Impact / Outcome
What changed, launched, improved, connected, or moved forward? Even early-stage outcomes count here.

C — SAMPLE ORBIT LANGUAGE BUILDER

Use this template as a starting point for every ORBIT entry narrative. Fill in the brackets with your specifics.

During [month / timeframe], [IU / team] supported [audience] through [activity]. This work aligned to Deliverable [letter] by [connection to SoW]. Evidence includes [artifact / evidence]. Impact included [outcome / change / next step].

Example Entries

DEL B
STEELS Professional Learning

During October, IU staff supported district science educators through a STEELS professional learning session focused on three-dimensional instruction and classroom implementation. This work aligned to Deliverable B by supporting standards implementation across regional districts. Evidence includes the agenda, slide deck, participant list, and follow-up resources. Impact included increased teacher readiness and identified next steps for district-level implementation.

DEL C
AI / CS Professional Learning

During November, IU staff facilitated an AI and computer science professional learning experience for educators and administrators. This work aligned to Deliverable C by supporting CS integration, AI literacy, and computational thinking connections. Evidence includes the session agenda, shared resources, and participant feedback. Impact included increased educator confidence and new district conversations around responsible AI use.

DEL E
STEM Ecosystem Partner Meeting

During January, IU staff convened regional education, workforce, and community partners to identify opportunities for STEM ecosystem collaboration. This work aligned to Deliverable E by strengthening cross-sector partnerships and connecting classroom learning to regional workforce needs. Evidence includes meeting notes, partner attendance, and action steps. Impact included new collaboration opportunities and clearer next steps for regional STEM programming.

Think in Evidence, Not Just Events

Strong reporting is not just listing activities. It captures what the work actually accomplished:

  • Educator impact — who was reached, what shifted in practice
  • Student reach — direct or indirect contact with learners
  • Workforce engagement — partners, employers, industry connections
  • Systems change — district plans, policies, or structural shifts
  • Resource creation — tools, guides, or materials that persist after the event
  • Collaboration growth — new partnerships, cross-IU connections, network depth
  • Implementation progress — evidence that work led to sustained follow-through
Good evidence tells the story of why the work mattered.

The SOW is more than a compliance document — it is a statewide coordination framework. Every PoC working their deliverables is simultaneously building a shared professional infrastructure for Pennsylvania STEM: shared resources, shared learning, shared evidence, shared momentum.

When you log ORBIT entries, attend workgroup meetings, contribute to the STEM Toolkit, or show up to a regional PLC — you are not just doing your job. You are contributing to a network that would not exist without each of its nodes showing up.
