WG-MATH // WORKGROUP PROFILE

Math Focus Workgroup

Creating statewide collaboration for IU math consultants and strengthening the "M" in STEM — through shared events, math identity work, data science integration, and a sustained professional learning network across Pennsylvania.

Lead: Kristina Fulton (IU13)
Deliverables: DEL G · DEL D
Active / Emerging STEM Integration

What This Group Does

Focus: This workgroup is evolving into a statewide math collaboration space — moving beyond individual IU silos toward shared infrastructure for math events, professional learning, and resources that every PA IU math consultant can draw on. The vision is a sustained network that persists beyond any one grant or leadership change.
WHY
Purpose & Intent
Strengthening the M in STEM — statewide
Core ▾

Math consultants across PA IUs often work in isolation — solving the same problems independently, duplicating resources, and missing the compound benefits of statewide collaboration. This workgroup builds the network that lets them share, align, and amplify their work.

The math-in-STEM framing matters too: math is often the forgotten letter. This group advocates for math's role in integrative STEM, connects math to data science and computational thinking, and makes the case that strong math identity is a prerequisite for STEM participation — especially for historically underserved students.

EVT
Math Events Coordination
Shared calendar and visibility across IUs
Active ▾

Building a shared layer for 2026–2027 IU math events — so consultants across the state can see what's happening, avoid scheduling conflicts, and connect their local work to a statewide calendar. Includes a newsletter events space for distribution across the network.

  • 2026–2027 IU Math Events sharing space
  • Newsletter events space for statewide distribution
  • Curating math events across IUs — conferences, summits, PLs
  • Building PAIU-wide math resource lists
ID
Math Identity & Narrative
Shifting who sees themselves as a math person
Active ▾

The "math people" narrative is one of the most damaging in education — it convinces students and teachers alike that math ability is fixed and innate. This workgroup takes math identity seriously as an equity issue, using narrative-forward tools to shift that story.

  • Counted Out — film screening connected to math identity and equity
  • Story Tables — math identity narrative project for students and educators
  • First in Math / Love Math Challenge — engagement and identity-building resources
  • Supporting math identity and narrative work across IUs
DS
Data Science Integration
Connecting math to real-world data and DS4E
Emerging ▾

Data science is the fastest-growing lane where math, computing, and real-world application converge. This workgroup connects IU math consultants to the DS4E movement, supporting data literacy, computational thinking integration, and the growing overlap between math and CS/AI work.

  • Data Science 4 Everyone (DS4E) Summit — supporting and connecting to the DS4E movement
  • Computational thinking and real-world data application
  • Connecting math to STEM, data science, and Del G
  • Overlap with PA Smart & CS on AI literacy and CT
FIN
Personal Finance & Applied Math
Real-world math connections for student relevance
Developing ▾

Building math connections to financial literacy and real-world application — supporting IUs in connecting math standards to personal finance, economic reasoning, and career-relevant quantitative skills. Strengthens the relevance argument for math with students and families.

  • Personal finance resources for IU math consultants
  • Connecting math standards to financial literacy frameworks
  • Career-relevant quantitative skills pathways
