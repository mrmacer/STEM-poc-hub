Remake Learning Days

Statewide coordination for Pennsylvania's Remake Learning Days festival — a network of IU Regional Leads working to grow events, support hosts, distribute materials, and connect districts to Career Ready PA programming.

Leads: Kelsi Wilcox Boyles (RIU6) · Sarah Brambley (IU8)
Cadence: Monthly, 60 min · Seasonal sprint cadence
Members: 9 IU representatives
Seasonal · Active Apr–May

What This Group Coordinates

Mission: Support the growth of Remake Learning Days across Pennsylvania — connecting IU Regional Leads, event hosts, school districts, Career Ready PA liaisons, and youth/parent ambassadors into a coordinated statewide festival.
EVENTS
Event Host Support
Coordinate host submissions, funding distribution, materials logistics, and communications for RLD events across all IU regions.
MKTG
Marketing & Materials
Posters, yard signs, rack cards, postcards, and county-wide flyers distributed through the IU to hosts. Multi-page flyers go directly to hosts.
CR PA
Career Ready PA
Backpack Challenge, Mega Events, sticker distribution, and artifact submission — coordinated between RLD Regional Leads and Career Ready Liaisons.
AMB
Ambassadors
Youth Ambassadors (140 apps received) and Parent/Teacher Ambassadors (120 apps) — application, selection, and deployment across regions.
DISC
DiscoverEd
Statewide challenge open to all 1st & 2nd grade classrooms. Aligned to STEELS, begins with a phenomenon to investigate.
VIRTUAL
Virtual Events
IU12/FrAY (Abbey) hosting virtual student events. Kelsi building a Smore of virtual events for the network to share out.

How It Works — Ops & Roles

OPS
Materials & Logistics Flow
How stuff gets from RLD to hosts — who does what
Operations ▾

The general rule: everything flows through the IU except the multi-page flyer, which goes directly to hosts (if requested).

  • Posters, yard signs, postcards, rack cards → sorted at IU → hosts pick up or IU delivers
  • Rack cards for CCIU: they print their own — order separately
  • Multi-page flyers: go directly from RLD to event hosts who requested them
  • IU notifies hosts when funding has been sent (this has been an ongoing communication gap to close)
Open logistics questions from the group:
  • Why can't IU3 pay event hosts directly rather than routing through individual IUs? (They take a fiscal manager cut)
  • Do we want lower funding for private events?
  • CCIU model worth stealing: use photography funds to send staff to events to capture content
  • Items were slow to arrive close to the May 1 deadline in 25-26 — can we get these out earlier next year?
  • Some IUs still waiting on stickers (Career Ready & NWPA Regional) as of April 2026
ROLES
RLD Regional Lead vs. Career Ready Liaison
Who owns what in the Mega Challenge ecosystem
Role Clarity ▾

Clarified via Cari's email from Laura Fridici and RLD national. The handoff point is form completion.

Who Owns
RLD Regional Lead Encourage everyone to participate. Share the Mega Challenge qualifier form with potential hosts. That's where your job ends on Mega Events.
Career Ready PA Liaison Takes over once the form is submitted. Handles: school artifact registration, written confirmation with host, supporting registered schools, must be at the event, sends stickers after, manages all artifact submission logistics.
PACK
Career Ready PA Backpack Challenge
Statewide student challenge · Each region running it differently
Active ▾

The Backpack Challenge is a Career Ready PA program running in parallel with RLD events. As of the April 2026 meeting, the group is still sorting out which schools and districts have registered.

  • Registration tracked via shared spreadsheet — check there for current participation
  • Each region is handling it differently — no unified playbook yet; this is a known gap
  • Career Ready Regional Leads are responsible for sharing the challenge and registration emails
  • Abbey (IU12/FrAY) also hosting virtual events for students tied to this work
DASH
Dashboard Idea (Proposed)
Unified visibility across all RLD tracks — still on the wish list
Proposed ▾

Surfaced in March 2025 — the group wants a single dashboard showing where each IU stands across all the moving parts. Nobody's built it yet.

Proposed buckets:
  • DiscoverEd status
  • Event Host registrations + funding status
  • Advertisement/marketing (quantities, delivery status)
  • Proclamations (by county)
  • Youth ambassadors (by region)
  • Parent/Teacher ambassadors
  • Grants
  • Videographer/photographer assignments
  • Total events by county
  • IU-specific asks with deadlines and contracts
  • Mega events / Backpack Challenge status
  • Host awards — where each IU is in the process

Each bucket should include: relevant email, flyer, and any forms. This would dramatically reduce the "where are we?" questions each meeting.
