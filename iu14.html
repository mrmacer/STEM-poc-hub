Identity

Mission & Regional Context

Mission

Provide high-quality resources, innovative services, and responsible leadership to enrich the educational and social services needs of children, families, schools, and the Berks County community.

Regional Context

Berks County's urban core (City of Reading) is a majority-minority, high-poverty area surrounded by rural and suburban communities. 16% of Berks households lack broadband; 30% of those connected need affordability/literacy help — driving the Digital Navigator initiative.

Signature Programs

Named Initiatives

01

Career Ready Berks

Countywide career readiness initiative connecting 18 school districts, 2 CTCs, post-secondary institutions, Greater Reading Chamber Alliance, Berks Workforce Development Board, BBEC, and 200+ employers. Provides 300+ career readiness experiences for K-12 students.

  • 70,000+ students connected to employer experiences
  • Flagged workforce/transition relevance
02

Digital Navigator Program

BCIU-led broadband equity initiative piloting in Hamburg and Tulpehocken; training digital navigators, forging community partnerships, creating work-study and apprenticeship opportunities for students.

  • Replicable model for rural IUs with broadband gaps
  • Supported by Berks County Broadband Workgroup
03

Berks STEM Connection

Named educator network connecting Berks County teachers for STEM professional learning and STEELS implementation support. Math and Science Partners network also active.

  • STEM Competitions offered as enrichment activities
  • STEELS implementation capacity cited as growing strength
04

Berks Online Learning (BOL)

Online course delivery platform serving students as alternative/supplemental instruction. Paraeducator credential programs, RBT pathways, and Guest Teacher Program as internal career pipelines.

Organizational Health

Strengths & Challenges

Strengths

  • Career Ready Berks — 200+ employer network, 300+ student experiences K-12
  • STEELS implementation capacity and Berks STEM Connection network
  • Digital Navigator broadband equity leadership
  • Highly qualified educators serving ELL, low-incidence disabilities, at-risk learners
  • 82 operating budgets — effective local/state/federal fiscal coordination
  • ECYEH Region 2 coordinator role for students experiencing homelessness

Challenges

  • Recruitment and retention of highly qualified staff — acutest for paraeducators, EC educators, SLPs
  • Digital equity gap — 16% households without broadband, 30% needing affordability/literacy help
  • Large geographic service area creating consistency and access challenges
  • Increased behavioral and mental health needs post-pandemic
  • Family engagement inconsistency across programs

Network Value

Cross-IU Collaboration Opportunities

Digital Equity / Broadband Navigator

BCIU14's Digital Navigator pilot is a replicable model for rural IUs facing similar broadband gaps — statewide model-sharing opportunity.

Career Ready Platform

The Career Ready Berks employer engagement platform (300+ experiences, 200+ businesses) could serve as a template for IUs building countywide career readiness ecosystems.

STEELS / STEM PD

BCIU14 explicitly names STEELS implementation capacity as a growing strength; could share resources or co-develop PD with IUs deepening STEELS work.

Educator Recruitment Pipeline

Internship expansion, university partnerships, RBT credentialing, Guest Teacher programs — statewide relevance given near-universal staffing crisis.

Plan Priorities

2024–2027 SMART Goals

01

Professional Growth (Instructional Support Staff)

Increase paraeducator/instructional support staff participation in professional learning by 25% over three years.

02

Employee Recruitment & Retention

Reduce educator and instructional support staff vacancy rate by 15%.

03

Mental / Behavioral Health

Increase educator access to mental/behavioral health strategies, tools, and coaching by 25% (survey-reported).

04

Family Engagement

Increase family engagement opportunities in early childhood and special education programs by 15% from baseline.
