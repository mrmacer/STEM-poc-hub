Science Workgroup

The statewide home for STEELS implementation support — building teacher and administrator PLCs, developing assessment tools, surfacing classroom implementation examples, and sustaining the science leadership network across Pennsylvania.

Leads: Colleen Epler-Ruths · Jen Sciacca (CAIU)
Cadence: 1st Tuesday monthly, 12–1pm
Deliverable: DEL B · DEL A
Active / High Momentum

What This Group Does

Focus: This workgroup is the statewide engine for STEELS implementation — supporting PoCs as they help districts, schools, and teachers navigate 3D teaching and learning, new assessment approaches, and the leadership infrastructure needed to make STEELS stick beyond the first year of rollout.
WHY
Purpose & Intent
The statewide STEELS implementation network
Core ▾

STEELS is the most significant science standards shift in Pennsylvania in a generation. The Science Workgroup exists to ensure PoCs across all 29 IUs are supported in helping their districts make that shift — not just in compliance, but in actual instructional change.

The workgroup brings together science consultants to share what's working, surface strong classroom examples, build shared resources, and sustain a statewide professional learning community that goes deeper than any single PaTTAN institute or WestEd CoP session can go.

PLC
Statewide STEELS PLCs
Teacher and administrator professional learning communities
High Momentum ▾

The workgroup runs parallel PLCs for teachers and administrators — recognizing that instructional change requires both classroom-level support and building/district-level leadership alignment. These aren't one-and-done sessions; they're designed as ongoing communities that build relationships and shared practice over time.

  • Statewide STEELS teacher PLCs — 3D teaching focus
  • Statewide STEELS administrator PLCs — look-fors and leadership support
  • Identifying teachers for PLC panels and case studies
  • Surfacing strong classroom implementation examples statewide
FRMK
Science Curriculum Framework
Statewide curriculum alignment workgroup
Active ▾

The Science Curriculum Framework Workgroup addresses a critical gap: districts need guidance on curriculum adoption and alignment to STEELS, but there's no single statewide answer. This subgroup gathers data on what districts are adopting, surfaces strong curriculum examples, and develops guidance IUs can use in their local curriculum conversations.

  • Gathering science curriculum and resource adoption data across IUs
  • Developing curriculum alignment guidance for STEELS
  • Connecting IU science consultants to shared curriculum work
  • Supporting districts in evaluating instructional materials
LEAD
PA Science Leadership Collaborative
Cross-sector science leadership network
Active ▾

The PA Science Leadership Collaborative connects science leaders across IUs, PaTTAN, WestEd, and the broader statewide network. It's the layer above workgroup meetings where strategy, advocacy, and cross-sector coordination happen — ensuring STEELS work has the leadership infrastructure to be sustained.

  • Cross-IU science leadership coordination
  • Connecting PoC science work to PaTTAN and WestEd CoP
  • Advocacy for STEELS implementation resources and policy
  • Sustaining science leadership beyond grant cycles
ASMT
Assessment & Implementation Tools
Tools that make STEELS visible in classrooms
Resources ▾

Assessment in 3D science is one of the hardest shifts for teachers and districts. The workgroup has developed and curated tools that help educators understand what STEELS should look like in classrooms — and how to assess it without defaulting to recall-based testing.

  • STEELS Assessment Toolkit — resources for building STEELS-aligned assessments
  • Classroom Implementation Descriptors — what 3D teaching looks like at different levels
  • Foundation Boxes — anchoring resources for STEELS storylines
  • Science discourse resources — supporting argument from evidence and sense-making
  • Possible shared async STEELS 101 modules for new PoCs
101
Async STEELS 101 Modules
On-demand onboarding for new PoCs and teachers
In Development ▾

New PoCs and teachers entering STEELS work mid-cycle don't have time to wait for the next live PLC session. The shared async STEELS 101 module concept gives them a self-paced foundation — covering the key shifts, the framework, and what IU support looks like — so they arrive at live sessions ready to go deeper.

  • Self-paced STEELS foundations modules
  • Shared across IUs to reduce duplication
  • Designed for both new PoCs and new-to-STEELS teachers
  • Status: concept in development — being scoped by the workgroup
