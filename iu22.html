Mission & Vision

Mission

Bucks County IU is dedicated to compassionately supporting all learners, families, school districts, and the broader community through innovative teaching, experiential learning, responsive leadership, and effective business solutions. Their vision: strengthen inclusive cultures where all learn, flourish, and contribute.

Regional Identity

  • Executive Director: Mark Hoffman
  • Serves 13 public school districts, nonpublic schools, 3 CTE schools, and PDE across 200+ schools
  • Divisions: Special Education, Early Childhood/Head Start, Technology, Strategic Services, Education Foundation
  • Named Supervisor of STEAM Education role
  • Well-established Fab Lab Center with documented growth; Mobile Fab Lab reaching 25,000+ students
  • Rated "Exemplary" on community/business partnerships and high-expectations culture
STEM Signals & Technology Posture
Makerspaces

Fab Lab Center

Fixed-location Fab Lab serving 400+ summer campers and 3,000+ students during the school year. Documented demand exceeds current capacity — scaling the program is an explicit plan priority challenge.

Mobile STEAM

Mobile Fab Lab

Travels to schools via week-long residencies; has served over 25,000 public and nonpublic Bucks County students. Replicable architecture for reaching students beyond a central location.

STEAM Education

Supervisor of STEAM Education

A dedicated STEAM Education supervisor role exists within the IU, demonstrating institutional commitment to STEAM as a named organizational function. Fab Lab Camp coordination is a primary responsibility.

Digital Systems

Technology Division Modernization

Moving purchase orders, travel reimbursement, and payroll from paper/email to digital platforms. PowerSchool SIS, Oracle HCM Cloud, and an LMS for async PD delivery are being actively implemented.

AI / Technology

AI Not Yet Integrated

AI-specific initiatives are not named in this plan. Technology workshops educate staff on existing systems. Emerging technology strategy not explicitly articulated — this may be addressed in supplemental plans.

Workforce / Transition

Adult Services Expansion

Active plan to survey community needs and launch pilot adult programs. PA Careerlink and Bucks County OVR referenced. College/university partnerships being strengthened for internship and staff pipelines.

Named Initiatives & Programs
01

Fab Lab Center Summer Camp

Dedicated summer STEM/making enrichment with 400+ participants. Serves public and nonpublic school students from across Bucks County in a fixed-location makerspace environment.

02

Mobile Fab Lab Residencies

Week-long school residencies bringing maker and STEAM experiences directly to students across the county. Over 25,000 students reached — the most cited outreach number in the plan.

03

"Learn, Flourish, Contribute" Vision Campaign

Internal and external storytelling and culture initiative tied to the organizational vision. Designed to build stakeholder identification with mission across all 200+ schools and community partners.

04

Back to the Future Protocol

Internal structured reflection process used to identify current staff challenges and future aspirations, forming the foundation for strategic planning across IU divisions.

05

Early Learning Center Family Engagement

Fall Festival, Spring Fling, and expanded Center Committee Meetings to deepen family participation in Head Start and Early Childhood programs across the county.

06

Adult Services Pilot Program

Structured community needs assessment leading to launch of pilot adult programming, coordinated by the new Supervisor of Adult Programs and Services, with PA Careerlink as a resource partner.

Strengths & Challenges

Regional Strengths

  • High stakeholder satisfaction from LEA leaders and PK-12 stakeholders
  • Nearly 700 PD opportunities and 15,000+ registrations in a single year
  • 100+ CPE courses offered annually
  • Fab Lab / Mobile Fab Lab participation in documented growth
  • Community and business partnerships rated "Exemplary"
  • High expectations culture rated "Exemplary"
  • Mission-connected culture across the organization

Challenges & Gaps

  • Fab Lab scaling — evolving programs to meet increased demand
  • Adult services gap — gap analysis underway; pilot programs not yet launched
  • Long-range planning infrastructure immature; identified as a priority
  • Standard Operating Procedures inconsistent across divisions
  • Digital platform adoption — transitioning from paper-based processes
  • Data-driven resource allocation not yet consistently implemented
Plan Priorities
A

Mission, Vision & Values

By 2028, every community member actively embodies and promotes the IU mission and vision in daily work — driven by the "Learn, Flourish, Contribute" campaign and internal storytelling infrastructure.

B

Communications & Storytelling

Enhanced storytelling and advocacy amplifies mission and vision across all platforms by 2028, including Fab Lab reach documentation and community partner engagement metrics.

C

Supports & Services Expansion

Enhance family engagement in early childhood programs, expand adult services via gap analysis and pilots, and strengthen school-age services and PD reach by 2028.

D

Data Systems Implementation

Fully implement Oracle HCM Cloud, PAETEP, and PowerSchool SIS for improved staff evaluation, data accuracy, and operational reporting across all IU divisions.

E

Long-Range Planning

Conduct needs assessments and implement strategic improvements in operations, recruitment, and technology infrastructure to build mature long-range planning capacity by 2028.

Cross-IU Collaboration Opportunities
🚗

Mobile Fab Lab Model

25,000+ students reached via mobile residencies — a replicable architecture that rural and suburban IUs could study or adapt. Strong statewide STEM Toolkit conversation candidate.

💻

LMS-Based SpEd PD

Using LMS for live and async special education professional learning is scalable across IUs with shared staffing and certification challenges — cross-IU coordination could reduce costs.

🏫

University Intern Pipeline

Internship-to-hiring pipeline model for special education and related services staff addresses a statewide workforce challenge — design artifacts could be shared with other IUs.

📊

Adult Services Gap Analysis

Structured stakeholder survey and focus group methodology for adult services could serve as a model for IUs building or expanding workforce transition programs from a low baseline.
