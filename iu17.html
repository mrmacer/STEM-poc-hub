Identity

Mission & Regional Context

Mission

Provide, create, and facilitate high-quality educational solutions across Bradford, Lycoming, Sullivan, and Tioga counties grounded in integrity, respect, communication, and safety.

Entrepreneurial Model

BLaST operates a fee-for-service model rather than a district assessment model, making competitive grants and fee-for-service arrangements critical to financial sustainability. An entrepreneurial, grant-seeking posture defines the organization's DNA.

Initiatives

Named Programs & Workforce Pipeline

01

Recruit, Prepare, Retain Initiative

Systemic staffing pipeline strategy addressing educator shortage through alternative certification pathways, "grow your own" models, career ladders, and internship partnerships with high schools, colleges, and universities.

  • Alternative certification pathways in development
  • Career ladder and on-the-job training programs planned
02

Explore (Job Coaching) Program

Work-based job coaching for students with disabilities; explicit transition-to-work relevance. ELECT (Education Leading to Employment and Career Training) provides career training for parents and families.

03

Empowered Leadership Initiative

Internal organizational development initiative to build distributed leadership capacity across all four divisions — Educational Planning, Student Services, Technology, and Management Services.

  • Culture/climate survey tools and book studies
  • Division-based empowerment structures
04

Data-Informed Systems Initiative

Organization-wide audit and mapping of all data systems, KPI establishment, and consistent data-driven decision-making processes. Possible emerging data science alignment for PAIMS.

  • eQUIP Online Learning platform for Act 48 PD
  • Penn Data services offered to member districts
Organizational Health

Strengths & Challenges

Strengths

  • Mission and vision well-understood across all staff
  • Entrepreneurial funding model actively pursuing competitive grants
  • Strategic collaboration between Curriculum Consultants and TAC Specialists
  • IU 17 consultants participate on PAIU/PDE statewide networks
  • High-quality professional learning, training, and resources available

Challenges

  • Statewide educator shortage hits BLaST acutely in rural region
  • Distributed leadership gaps — inconsistent decision-making protocols
  • Data-informed decision making: staff lack shared understanding of key metrics
  • Heavy reliance on State System of Support funding creates financial vulnerability
  • Community awareness of IU is low — stakeholders unclear on what BLaST offers
  • System utilization fidelity: existing tools not used consistently

Equity & Inclusion

UDL, ELL, behavioral health, and whole-child service portfolio are central to BLaST's identity. No STEM programs, makerspaces, or science curriculum initiatives named in plan. Plan is almost entirely inward-facing — organizational capacity, human capital, data infrastructure.

Network Value

Cross-IU Collaboration Opportunities

Alternative Certification Pipelines

BLaST's explicit focus on non-traditional pathways, career ladders, and internship partnerships could be a model for rural IUs facing similar educator pipeline constraints.

Empowered Leadership Models

Culture/climate survey tools and division-based empowerment structures could align with or inform PAIMS leadership development conversations across the network.

Educator Staffing via ESS

BLaST's partnership with Educational Staffing Solutions and "grow your own" models may be of interest to IUs facing similar rural staffing pressures.

Data Systems / KPI Infrastructure

The Internal Data Systems Matrix initiative — auditing and mapping data systems with KPIs — is a potential model for statewide PAIMS data governance conversations.

Plan Priorities

2024–2027 SMART Goals

01

Empowered Leadership

Build collective capacity for distributed, empowered leadership across all IU divisions through common definitions, professional learning, and division-based opportunities.

02

Recruit, Prepare, Retain

Implement systemic strategies to recruit staff through alternative pathways, build "grow your own" and mentorship models, and develop career ladders and on-the-job training to improve retention.

03

Data-Informed Systems

Audit and map all internal data systems, establish KPIs, and build consistent data-driven decision-making processes communicated across all divisions and to external stakeholders.
