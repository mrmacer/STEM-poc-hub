Capital Area Intermediate Unit

CAIU serves students ages 3–21 across the Capital Area including adjudicated youth at Loysville Youth Development Center and students at Penn State Health Milton Hershey Hospital. Project SEARCH and SCPaWorks anchor workforce transition programming.

Identity

Mission & Vision

Mission

Provide innovative support and services in partnership with schools, families, and communities to build capacity and model courageous leadership across the Capital Area.

Vision

Recognized as a trusted and influential partner in achieving life-changing outcomes. CAIU serves students ages 3–21 at multiple sites including Hill Top Academy, Penn State Health Milton Hershey Hospital, Loysville Youth Development Center, and Diakon Center Point.

Signature Programs

Named Initiatives & Workforce Programs

01

Project SEARCH

Community-based transition-to-employment program located at Penn State Health Milton Hershey Medical Center. Community partner: Danielle Iovino. Hospital-embedded model is nationally recognized and potentially replicable.

  • Strong workforce/transition signal
  • SCPaWorks also on Steering Committee (Lynda Morris)
02

Comprehensive Mentoring Program

Organization-wide mentoring initiative for all CAIU employees — mentor/mentee identification, stipend criteria, and participant playbook. Timeline: 2024–2027.

  • Potentially exportable model for IU staff mentoring statewide
  • Explicit goal to evaluate and revise by June 30, 2027
03

Early Learning Continuum

Early Intervention (ages 3–5), Early Intervention Connections (EIC) community forums, Rapid Response Team for preschool/daycare settings, and "Ready for School Ready to Success" (United Way partnership).

04

ELECT Program

Serves young adults who are pregnant or parenting — transition support to employment and higher education. Adjudicated youth at Loysville and Diakon represent specialized alternative education expertise.

Organizational Health

Strengths & Challenges

Strengths

  • Strong, positive, trust-based relationships with member districts
  • Technology Department support valued by those served
  • Strong network of safety experts
  • Conferences, workshops, and networking offered with equity practices focus
  • Strong mental health professionals interpreting data and tailoring services
  • Effective two-way communication with stakeholders (rated Exemplary)

Challenges

  • Rapid technological advancement makes maintaining secure cyber environments difficult
  • Recruiting and retaining fully credentialed staff in changing landscape
  • Building more personalized professional learning opportunities
  • Developing equitable practices self-audit framework across all programs
  • Growing student mental health needs require expanded screening and response systems
  • Shifting educational landscapes require frequent short-term adjustments

Equity & Inclusion

Explicit equity and belonging language throughout; bias acknowledgment, inclusive programming, and equitable practices are core values. Project SEARCH and SCPaWorks are workforce hooks. No STEM, CS, AI, or makerspace content in plan.

Network Value

Cross-IU Collaboration Opportunities

Project SEARCH Replication

Hospital-embedded transition-to-employment program — collaboration point for IUs seeking Project SEARCH host sites or model guidance.

Staff Mentoring Framework

CAIU is explicitly building a comprehensive mentoring framework with stipend criteria and participant playbook — potential statewide model for IU staff mentoring.

Mental Health & Behavioral Supports

Strong mental health professional infrastructure including Rapid Response Team for preschool/daycare — potential model for IUs building Tier 2 behavioral consultation capacity.

Equitable Practices Framework

CAIU's stated need for an equity self-audit framework aligns with statewide Equity/Inclusion workgroup efforts — potential co-development opportunity.

Plan Priorities

2024–2027 SMART Goals

01

Recruitment & Retention

Evaluate, refine, and measure CAIU recruitment and retention efforts to positively impact staffing outcomes across all program areas.

02

Comprehensive Mentoring Program

Evaluate and revise the comprehensive mentoring program for all CAIU employees by June 30, 2027.

03

Comprehensive Professional Learning

Foster continuous improvement and develop a culture of lifelong learning aligned to organizational needs by June 30, 2027.

04

Leadership Capacity Building

Comprehensively analyze and improve supervision and evaluation practices; leverage role-specific collaborative meetings to enhance leadership capacity for internal staff and external partners.
