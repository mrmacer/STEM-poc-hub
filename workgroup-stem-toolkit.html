About This Group

Mission: Create equitable access for all Pennsylvanians to high-quality educational resources. The STEM Toolkit Workgroup supports the PA STEM Toolkit as a statewide clearinghouse of vetted, accessible resources connected to STEM in PA initiatives — including UDL, STEELS, Computer Science, 3D teaching and learning, Esports, Student-Centered Learning, and other statewide priorities.
WHY
Purpose & Strategic Role
Why this workgroup exists in the network
Core ▾

Every PoC and TaC eventually hears the same question from teachers: "Where do I start?" The STEM Toolkit is the statewide answer — a curated, navigable resource system that gives educators an on-ramp to 3D science, CS, UDL, and STEM integration without requiring PoCs to build everything from scratch at the IU level.

The workgroup's deeper mission is to make the Toolkit usable for the hardest-to-reach users — emergency-certified science teachers, reassigned staff, special education educators, and teachers who don't identify as STEM people. That equity lens shapes every organizational and design decision the group makes.

2025–2026 Focus
This year's organizing priority
Current ▾

Create resources aligned to user profiles to support more personalized and sustainable training.

The workgroup is designing three specific user pathways so educators see themselves in the Toolkit — and can quickly find what applies to their situation:

🆕
Teacher new to science without certification — an emergency-certified or long-term sub who needs an immediate starting point and basic support
🔄
Certified teacher with a new science assignment — an experienced teacher who is newly responsible for a course they haven't taught before
♿
Teacher in a special needs classroom — a special education educator or inclusion teacher who needs STEELS-aligned, UDL-ready resources designed for their students

Toolkit Pathway Model

Resources in the PA STEM Toolkit are organized by complexity and user readiness — four levels that help educators self-select an entry point without needing to know the whole framework first.

🩹
Duct Tape
Need-it-now support for educators who need an immediate starting point. Minimal context, maximum utility — built for survival mode.
🧰
Tool Box
Foundational instructional elements, STEELS 101, and basic navigation support. Builds understanding alongside immediate usefulness.
⚡
Power Tools
Deeper implementation resources — phenomena, foundation boxes, learning progressions, evidence statements, assessment toolkit, and OER authoring resources.
🏗
Contractor
More robust programs, larger offerings, or deeper implementation support. For educators and systems doing sustained, high-complexity STEM work.

Active Projects

START
Start Here Folder
OER Commons entry point for new users
Active ▾

The Start Here folder inside OER Commons is the front door for educators coming to the Toolkit for the first time. The workgroup is building and refining this space to be genuinely usable — not just a list of links, but a guided entry into the right resources for each user type.

  • Clear pathway entry points for the three user profiles
  • "How to Use the Toolkit" resource — scoped and in development
  • Navigation flowchart for self-directed users
  • OER Commons / Open Author training and support
RORG
Resource Reorganization
Spreadsheet redesign and four-category realignment
Active ▾

A major workgroup effort: taking the existing resource inventory and reallocating resources into the Duct Tape / Tool Box / Power Tools / Contractor framework. This makes the Toolkit navigable by user readiness rather than requiring educators to understand the full architecture before they can find anything useful.

  • Resource spreadsheet redesign for clarity and usability
  • Reallocating existing resources into the four pathway levels
  • Aligning training items with user profiles
  • Removing or archiving outdated materials
MRKT
Teacher-Facing Marketing
Making the Toolkit visible and usable for teachers
Developing ▾

The Toolkit is only as useful as the number of educators who know it exists and know how to enter it. The workgroup is building teacher-facing marketing resources that PoCs and TaCs can use to introduce the Toolkit in professional learning sessions, onboarding, and IU communications.

  • Teacher-facing marketing materials for the Toolkit
  • One-pagers and slide templates for PoC/TaC use
  • TaC / STEM PoC implementation pathway conversations
  • Possible audio/video support for key resources
EQT
SpEd & Equity Pathways
Resources for special education, UDL, and inclusive STEM
Developing ▾

Building pathways for the educators who most often get left out of STEM resource design: special education teachers, inclusion educators, and teachers supporting multilingual learners. This connects to PaTTAN / DLM partnerships and the Equity, TaC & Inclusion Workgroup.

  • Connect with PaTTAN / DLM partners around SpEd science resources
  • Build pathways for emergency-certified or reassigned teachers
  • Consider AI-supported navigation pathways for complex resource sets
  • UDL integration across Toolkit levels
