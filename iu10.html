Identity

Mission & Vision

Mission

Provide innovative and engaging services to support equitable access to educational opportunities across member LEAs.

Vision

Strategic partnerships that promote equity, access, and innovation — with community engagement and stakeholder outreach as growth areas for this plan cycle.

Initiatives

Named Programs & Technology Focus

01

Equitable Technology Skills Hub

Asynchronous professional learning hub for staff to access ongoing technology training, developed via Moodle and Padlet by the Curriculum & Innovation Department.

  • Equity of access for all staff roles
  • Bi-annual monitoring of participation
02

Paperless Documentation System

Multi-year initiative converting all internal job-related forms, monthly documents, and financial accounting to electronic processes via FIS/SCView system.

  • Target: complete by August 2026
  • Led by the Business Manager
03

Communication Overhaul

Three-year initiative to improve internal messaging, website rebranding, social media presence, and external stakeholder communications; led by Technology Director and Cabinet.

  • Annual stakeholder survey and interview cycle
  • Community Connections aligned to needs data
Organizational Health

Strengths & Challenges

Strengths

  • Collaborative planning environment across staff and stakeholders
  • Coordination of fiscal resources from local, state, and federal programs
  • Culture of high expectations for success across all stakeholders
  • SEL/behavioral support via TaC staff and PBIS
  • Skillful technology use within individual programs (despite system-level gaps)

Challenges

  • Internal and external communication systems fragmented — root cause of organizational challenges
  • Technology utilization at systems level is an ongoing gap
  • Onboarding new staff to ensure service continuity
  • Expanding partnerships with local businesses and community organizations (underdeveloped)
  • Stakeholder engagement remains difficult across the region

Equity & Inclusion

Equity of access is the explicit core of mission, vision, and technology goals. No STEM, science, CS, makerspace, competition, or environmental literacy signals found in this plan. CIU 10 is a low-STEM-engagement partner for this plan cycle.

Network Value

Cross-IU Collaboration Opportunities

Equitable Tech Access Models

CIU 10's async technology hub (Moodle/Padlet) for staff PD infrastructure could be shared with IUs developing similar equitable access programs.

Communication & Branding Frameworks

Other IUs navigating website rebuilds and social media launches may benefit from CIU 10's documented communication overhaul process once implemented.

The annual survey/interview cycle for community stakeholder needs assessment is a replicable model for IUs seeking to systematize community engagement.

Plan Priorities

2024–2027 SMART Goals

01

Paperless Documentation

Transition all internal job-related, monthly, and financial forms to electronic documentation using the FIS/SCView system by August 2026.

02

Equitable Technology Skills

Provide all staff equitable access to technology professional learning via an asynchronous hub (Moodle/Padlet) with bi-annual monitoring.

03

Community Needs & Communication

Annually update the IU's comprehensive communication plan based on stakeholder surveys and interviews; develop improved internal and external communication systems over three years.
