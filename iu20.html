Mission & Vision

Mission

CIU 20 centers its work on building positive relationships, responding to stakeholder needs, and demonstrating measurable results through student-focused services and programs. The organization envisions itself as a vibrant, child-centered institution capable of reaching students, families, and educators wherever they are and in whatever form they need support.

Regional Identity

  • Serves Monroe, Northampton, and Carbon counties in eastern PA
  • Headquarters: 6 Danforth Drive; plan approved February 2024
  • Anchor districts include Bethlehem Area, Pocono Mountain, Northampton Area, Nazareth Area
  • Partner: East Stroudsburg University (Steering Committee)
  • Strategic posture: course-correcting post-pandemic curriculum gap; internally focused on instructional coherence and retention
Program Signals & Focus Areas
Literacy

Structured Literacy / ELA Initiative

Multi-year effort to implement evidence- and standards-based ELA curriculum using a structured literacy framework across all IU programs by end of 2026–2027. Uses PA SAS frameworks and DLM alignment as anchors.

Professional Learning

Acadience Essentials Training

Professional development program for teachers and administrators on structured literacy assessment tools, supporting consistent data-driven reading instruction across IU-served programs.

Staff Retention

Stay Interview & EIB Strategy

Structured retention approach including Stay Interviews, satisfaction surveys, and an Employee Inclusion and Belonging (EIB) framework. Targets a 10% reduction in voluntary resignations by end of plan period.

Equity / Inclusion

Equitable Standards-Based Curriculum

Emphasis on ensuring all learners — including those with IEPs and diverse learning needs — access evidence-based, standards-aligned instruction. DLM (Dynamic Learning Maps) explicitly integrated.

AI / Technology

Technology Not Explicitly Addressed

No AI initiatives, edtech platforms, or digital learning programs are named in this plan. Technology referenced only in context of retention surveys and an internal Employee Intranet platform.

STEM

STEM Not Addressed in Plan

No STEM programs, makerspaces, science curriculum, competitions, or engineering activities are mentioned in this comprehensive plan. IU 20's 2024–2027 cycle focuses entirely on literacy and internal organizational health.

Strengths & Challenges

Regional Strengths

  • Coordination of local, state, and federal fiscal resources
  • Flexible and customizable professional development for each LEA
  • Strong ongoing communication — 8 of 9 surveyed LEAs satisfied with IU services
  • Strong supervisor-to-staff communication; effective classroom teaming
  • Strong family communication — families report feeling like partners
  • Improved onboarding process in place; new hire PD series functioning

Challenges & Gaps

  • Staffing shortages and substitute coverage (pandemic-exacerbated)
  • Voluntary resignations — targeted for 10% reduction
  • Lack of consistent, evidence-based curriculum across all programs
  • Standards-aligned assessment infrastructure incomplete
  • Administrator capacity as instructional leaders needs development
  • Previous comp plan curriculum goal derailed by pandemic — this plan is the course correction
Plan Priorities
A

ELA Curriculum — Structured Literacy

By end of 2026–2027, all IU programs will implement an evidence- and standards-based curriculum using a structured literacy framework, with SAS alignment and DLM resources embedded.

B

Staff Retention

By end of 2026–2027, IU 20 will reduce voluntary staff resignations by 10% overall through Stay Interviews, EIB initiatives, and improved internal communication and belonging infrastructure.

Cross-IU Collaboration Opportunities
📚

Structured Literacy Framework

CIU 20's multi-year ELA curriculum with scope/sequence, assessment calendars, and Acadience + DLM alignment could serve as a shareable model for IUs developing structured literacy in special education settings.

👤

Staff Retention Strategy Design

The Stay Interview, EIB framework, and HR-driven retention survey model is directly applicable to the near-universal IU challenge of early-career turnover — replicable across the state.

📌

SAS Curriculum Alignment

Active use of Pennsylvania's SAS frameworks as a curriculum writing anchor is directly replicable and could support cross-IU curriculum committee work on standards alignment.
