STEM Lending Libraries Workgroup

Infrastructure for equitable STEM access — building a statewide platform for lending libraries, equipment sharing, and resource distribution so every student can experience hands-on STEM regardless of district resources.

Leads: Sarah Brambley (IU8) · Christina Sanders (IU5)
Cadence: 1st Tuesday monthly, 2pm
Active / Resource Infrastructure

What This Group Does

Focus: This workgroup functions as infrastructure for equitable STEM access. Every IU has STEM equipment sitting in closets or used only occasionally — the lending library model creates systems to share those resources across schools, districts, and regions so more students get hands-on experiences.
WHY
Purpose & Intent
What we're building and why it matters
Core ▾

Equipment access is an equity issue. Schools and districts with fewer resources — less funding, fewer specialized teachers, smaller budgets — often have less access to the hands-on STEM materials that make the standards meaningful. Lending libraries shift this dynamic.

The statewide platform vision: a searchable, shared system where IUs can list what they have, schools can request equipment, and the logistics of checkout, return, and maintenance are streamlined across the network. Connected to T&E, CS, and EL&S workgroups, and potentially to AT devices and inclusive STEM access.

PLAT
Statewide Platform
Shared inventory, checkout, and logistics system
In Progress ▾

The statewide STEM lending library platform is the long-term infrastructure goal — a shared system where IUs can list inventory, schools can search and request resources, and the checkout and return process is manageable at scale.

  • Searchable statewide inventory of STEM kits and equipment
  • Checkout and return logistics across IU regions
  • Maintenance and refresh cycles for shared materials
  • Connecting equipment to lesson examples and ORBIT evidence
EQT
Equity & Access
Connecting resources to the students who need them most
Equity Lens ▾

Lending libraries are an equity intervention as much as a logistics solution. This workgroup keeps the equity lens central — making sure sharing systems prioritize access for under-resourced schools and regions, not just convenience for those who already have the most.

  • Prioritizing access for under-resourced districts and schools
  • Possible connections to AT devices and inclusive STEM
  • Aligning resource sharing to Del G equity and access goals
  • Supporting multilingual learner and SpEd-accessible STEM materials
NET
Workgroup Connections
Where lending library work intersects with other groups
Network ▾

Lending libraries connect across multiple workgroups and deliverables depending on what's being shared:

  • T&E Workgroup — physical engineering and fabrication equipment
  • CS / PAsmart — robotics, drones, coding kits, maker materials
  • EL&S — field science equipment, watershed kits, environmental tools
  • STEM Toolkit — resource access aligns with OER Commons distribution
  • Equity / TaC — AT device access and inclusive STEM materials

Active Projects

CURRENT
Statewide Lending Library Platform Development
Working toward a shared, searchable inventory platform for STEM lending libraries across IUs. Includes checkout logistics, return tracking, and resource refresh planning.
IN PROGRESS
Equipment Sharing Systems
Documenting existing IU lending library models, sharing what's working, and developing replicable systems that other IUs can adopt or adapt.
DEVELOPING
ORBIT Evidence Mapping
Mapping lending library activity to deliverables so PoCs can document equipment use as ORBIT evidence — headcounts, lesson connections, and student experience documentation.

ORBIT & Deliverable Connections

DEL G — Access & Resources DEL B/C/F
ORBIT tip: Equipment checkouts, lending library events, and hands-on STEM experiences all generate Del G evidence. If the equipment connects to science (Del B), CS (Del C), or competitions (Del F) — document that context for stronger narrative.
