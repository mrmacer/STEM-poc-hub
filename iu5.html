Regional Context

Regional Identity Snapshot

Northwest Tri-County IU5 operates as a regional service agency prioritizing customer service, trust-building, and responsive support to its LEAs across a predominantly rural northwest Pennsylvania region. The IU demonstrated notable agility during COVID-19, rapidly shifting professional development to virtual delivery while maintaining or exceeding service targets. IU5 also serves as the Region 1 liaison for the Career Ready State Team, signaling a community-connected, workforce-aware posture. Internally, the organization emphasizes weekly departmental collaboration, perceptual data use, and a culture of continuous improvement.

Strengths + Challenges

Stated Strengths

  • Strong trust relationships with LEAs — described as exemplary through pandemic
  • Region 1 Career Ready State Team liaison — diverse stakeholder network
  • CIA department annually reviews and updates internal procedures

Organizational Direction

Plan Priorities

01

Staff & Leadership Empowerment

By June 30, 2024, CIA/School Improvement PD Instances increase 15% and staff climate surveys show a 3-point average increase.

02

Standards-Aligned Curriculum

Special Education updates supervisory model, increases EI Family Survey responses to 20%, moves 67% of EI students to typical preschool settings, and achieves 100% compliance on timelines.

03

TLE Seal

Innovative Technology Solutions will complete evidence portfolios across all 25 CoSN TLE areas and submit for TLE Seal evaluation by June 30, 2024.

04

Increase Outreach & Referrals

Fed/State Programs will improve outreach quality and increase referral opportunities by 15% to reach the remaining 40% of eligible learners.
