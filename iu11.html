Identity

Mission & Regional Identity

Mission & Vision

Connecting people, building communities, and improving lives. Vision: recognized leader and integral partner providing innovative, equitable, and accessible educational opportunities across south-central PA.

STEM Standing

TIU 11 explicitly self-identifies as one of the outstanding IUs in STEM Education statewide, with staff called upon to present at conferences. Despite small size, this is a high-identity STEM organization.

Organizational Health

Strengths & Challenges

Strengths

  • STEM state-recognized, conference-level expertise on staff
  • Personalized, district-specific instructional services
  • Strong relationships with member districts — actively sought out for PD
  • High-quality direct adult/professional services with strong district feedback
  • Culture of high expectations and reflective practice

Challenges

  • Staffing/recruitment — difficulty hiring fully credentialed, experienced staff
  • Internal communication and duplication of PD offerings across districts
  • Marketing/visibility — districts unaware of available services
  • ESL/EL support — small IU receives limited Title III funding
  • Adjudicated youth program scheduling constrained by court calendars
  • Community/business partnerships formally underdeveloped

Network Value

Cross-IU Collaboration Opportunities

STEM PD Export

State-recognized STEM staff may be an untapped PD resource for neighboring IUs — TIU 11's conference-level expertise should be surfaced through the PAIMS workgroup network.

Async PD Infrastructure

Small IUs with similar bandwidth constraints could share async course development platforms or co-develop content to amortize development cost.

ESL/EL challenge and Title III funding gaps mirror likely issues at other small-enrollment IUs; cross-IU coordination on Title III strategies is a statewide conversation opportunity.

Plan Priorities

2024–2027 SMART Goals

01

Network Meetings

Offer at least 3 networking PL opportunities beyond current offerings to 100% of member districts annually.

02

Banked Asynchronous Learning

Create 3 asynchronous PL opportunities for member districts by end of each school year.

03

Integrated Professional Development

Offer quarterly integrated on-site PD to 6 member districts using 2 of 3 TIU departments.

04

Hiring

Attend 5 career recruitment activities per year to hire qualified, credentialed personnel for all open positions.
