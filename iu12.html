Identity

Mission & Vision

Mission

Support partners, customers, and students in realizing their greatest potential through quality, innovative solutions and services.

Vision

Provider of choice for comprehensive service, support, and solutions — framing districts and families as partners and customers in co-creating educational solutions.

Organizational Health

Strengths & Challenges

Strengths

  • Listening & Sharing Forums, Coffee & Conversations, and "Are We Making Progress?" survey as active communication channels
  • Employee recognition culture: Employee of the Month, Annual Staff Awards, Educator Appreciation Week
  • Instructional coaching capacity with Jim Knight framework adoption
  • Multi-county service reach with county-by-county engagement structure

Challenges

  • Staff retention — particular concern for paraprofessionals
  • Paraprofessional preparation gap — dedicated onboarding, handbook, and mentoring in development
  • Community engagement consistency across counties
  • Family communication protocols lacking standardization
  • Curriculum infrastructure in development — K-8 scope, coaching systems still being built

Plan Priorities

2024–2027 SMART Goals

01

Two-Way Communication

By June 30, 2027, create, implement, monitor, and evaluate a strategic communications plan that enhances internal communication and strengthens two-way communication with external partners.

02

Curriculum & Coaching

By June 30, 2027, fully implement K-8 literacy and math curricula along with Dynamic Learning Maps and instructional coaching cycles.

03

Staff Retention

By June 30, 2027, implement processes to retain all staff, with particular focus on better preparing classroom support (paraprofessional) staff.
