Mission & Vision

Mission

DCIU's mission is to provide leadership in developing and delivering quality, cost-effective programs and services to school communities. Its vision: empowering partnerships that Drive, Create, Inspire, and Understand excellence in education — positioning the IU as a regional convener for professional development, school improvement, safety, and emerging technology.

Regional Identity

  • Executive Director: Dr. Maria Edelberg
  • Serves 15 public districts, 66+ nonpublic schools, 4 charter schools in Delaware County
  • Direct instruction to 12,000+ students in CTE, Student Services, and specialized programs
  • Significant organizational AI professional learning investment — cited as a strength
  • STEELS implementation noted as effective with LEAs
  • System in active transition: managing staffing pressures while modernizing around AI and evidence-based practice
STEM Signals & Technology Posture
STEM Integration

STEM Innovation and Inclusion Studio

Countywide STEM and assistive technology program impacting students and educators through STEM programs, co-curricular activities, and inclusive design. Explicitly cited as a plan strength. Outreach tracked via newsletter and advertising analytics.

STEELS

STEELS Implementation — Effective

STEELS standards implementation is explicitly named as effective in meeting LEA needs, placing DCIU in a leadership position among IUs navigating the transition from PSSA-era science to the new standards framework.

AI

Significant AI Professional Learning

Significant organizational investment in Artificial Intelligence and emerging technologies professional learning is named as both a strength and a challenge. DCIU is actively guiding organizational decision-making with AI-informed practice — while scrutinizing policy, security, and evidence-based integration.

Equity / UDL

STEM Equity Data Disaggregation

STEM data disaggregated by gender, special education status, and high-need district — DCIU is tracking equity dimensions within STEM delivery, not just aggregate participation. Inclusion Studio name directly reflects this commitment.

Instructional Leadership

Professional Learning Communities

PLCs established and functional across K-12 programs. Supervisors Academy supports administrator development. Job Alike groups expanding job-embedded PD countywide. PLC model being formalized as the central instructional improvement architecture.

Workforce / Transition

CTE Programs

Career and Technical Education is a core direct-instruction program area serving students across Delaware County. Specific credentials and programs not detailed in the comprehensive plan, but CTE is a named service line.

Named Initiatives & Programs
01

STEM Innovation and Inclusion Studio

Countywide STEM and assistive technology studio delivering programs, co-curricular activities, and professional learning to students and educators across Delaware County. Outreach tracked via analytics.

02

Supervisors Academy

Internal leadership development initiative growing the goals and instructional impact of K-12 services leaders — a core retention and administrator capacity strategy alongside formal PLC infrastructure.

03

Professional Learning Communities (PLCs)

Site-based data and instructional leadership model being formalized across DCIU programs, aligned to standards-based curriculum and consistent assessment implementation targets.

04

Job Alike Groups

Countywide educator cohort networking and professional development creating job-embedded learning opportunities and professional connection across 15 public districts and nonpublic schools.

05

A-TSI / TSI School Improvement Support

Targeted support for schools in improvement status — helping them collect, analyze, and act on improvement data in alignment with state accountability requirements.

Strengths & Challenges

Regional Strengths

  • PLCs established and functional across K-12 programs
  • Supervisors Academy supporting administrator development
  • STEM Innovation and Inclusion Studio impacting students and educators countywide
  • Significant organizational investment in AI and emerging technology professional learning
  • STEELS standards implementation cited as effective with LEAs
  • Job alike groups and job-embedded PD expanding countywide reach
  • Strong safety, security, and stakeholder engagement leadership

Challenges & Gaps

  • Staffing and retention — demand exceeds qualified applicants; top priority
  • Staff burnout and PD bandwidth — time, substitutes, and bandwidth are limiting factors
  • Emerging technology policy — technologies moving faster than policy development
  • Fiscal coordination across local, state, and federal resources
  • STEM TaC Hours — incorporating STEM into SoW goals is a named challenge
  • Increasing student behavioral complexity requiring updated programming frameworks
Plan Priorities
A

Recruitment

Audit and implement innovative recruitment strategies to increase applicant volume across DCIU — addressing the gap between staffing demand and qualified candidate pipeline.

B

Retention

Develop a data-informed retention plan with onboarding and engagement programming for new staff, built through a dedicated retention committee using structured data analysis.

C

Instructional Leadership

Establish a PLC-driven system for consistent standards-aligned curriculum and assessment implementation across DCIU's direct-instruction programs and LEA partnerships.

D

Program Frameworks for Complex Needs

Identify and implement evidence-based programs for students with complex academic and behavioral needs — responding to increasing student behavioral complexity across Delaware County programs.
