Regional Context

Regional Identity Snapshot

Riverview IU 6 is a mid-sized northwestern Pennsylvania intermediate unit serving 17 school districts, reaching over 21,000 students indirectly through district supports and approximately 3,800+ students through direct services. The IU operates across a wide continuum — from early intervention to transition planning — and anchors its regional role in special education, technical assistance, and professional development. Its operational identity is deeply collaborative, driven by job-alike networks and a strong consultative posture toward local education agencies. The plan's most pressing strategic challenge is a severe regional staffing shortage that threatens service continuity and expansion.

Regional Intelligence

Strengths + Challenges

Stated Strengths

  • Special Education continuum meeting student needs across all settings
  • Updated health and safety plan with job-alike meeting integration
  • Established system for continuous improvement and organizational coherence
  • Strong technical assistance utilized by districts and local educators
  • Ability to fulfill State System of Support obligations per PDE protocols
  • Data-driven resource allocation practices

Challenges / Opportunity Radar

  • Staffing shortage (CRITICAL) — "near impossible" to expand services
  • Recruitment/retention of fully credentialed staff — top priority
  • Substitute teacher shortages limiting PD participation
  • Stakeholder engagement and two-way communication gaps
  • Building administrator instructional leadership capacity
  • Fiscal resource coordination across programs
Organizational Direction

Plan Priorities

01

90% Fill Rate — Professional & Support Staff

Develop data-driven HR approach; implement Handshake and social media recruitment; build induction programming; reach 90% fill/retention year-over-year.

  • Year 1: 80% | Year 2: 85% | Year 3: 90%
02

90% Fill Rate — Supervisors & Administrators

Same data-driven HR approach applied specifically to supervisor/administrator pipeline.

  • 90% fill/retention rate year-over-year target
Network Contributions

Cross-IU Collaboration Opportunities

Shared HR/Recruitment Strategies

RIU6's formal, data-driven staffing approach (Handshake, induction programs, exit interview analysis) could be a model or shared framework for rural IUs facing similar pipeline challenges.

Transition Coordinator Networking

RIU6 facilitates a regional Transition Coordinator Network; potential alignment with IUs developing or expanding transition ecosystems statewide.

Dual-Track Technology Model

RIU6 explicitly separates infrastructure-side tech (RWAN, hardware) from instructional technology (application, teaching with tools) in two distinct networks — worth sharing with IUs building tech integration infrastructure.
