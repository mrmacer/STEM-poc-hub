THE STORY OF THE NETWORK

The PA STEM PoC Network did not begin as a finished system. It grew through real statewide needs: grant support, computer science expansion, crisis response, standards implementation, ecosystem building, and now AI-era transformation. This hub exists because the network itself evolved from isolated regional work into shared statewide infrastructure.

From managing programs → to building Pennsylvania’s STEM innovation infrastructure.
FOUNDATION 2018–2019

PAsmart, SCRIPT, and the First Statewide STEM Support Layer

The early STEM PoC Network formed around PAsmart grants, district support, and the need to help schools understand what high-quality STEM and computer science implementation could look like across Pennsylvania.

  • PAsmart grants created the first statewide coordination pressure point
  • Carnegie STEM Pathway helped districts diagnose STEM readiness
  • SCRIPT training supported K–12 computer science planning
  • STEM Toolkit conversations began shifting from “activities” to vetted instructional resources
  • IUs started acting as statewide connectors, not just regional service providers
What changed: District support became more coherent, and the network began forming a shared language for STEM implementation.
RESILIENCE 2020

Remote Learning, Human-First Support, and Lending Library Reinvention

The pandemic forced the network to pivot quickly. The PoC meetings became more than updates — they became a statewide support system for educators navigating uncertainty, remote learning, technology access, and safe hands-on STEM.

  • Human-first check-ins became essential to every meeting
  • Remote learning and virtual STEM support moved to the center of the work
  • Lending libraries had to adapt around sanitization, logistics, and access
  • Equity and access became immediate operational questions, not aspirational ones
  • The Statement of Work began turning informal support into clearer deliverables
What changed: The network proved it could respond to crisis, not just manage programs.
INFRASTRUCTURE 2021–2022

From Programs to Systems

After the crisis response, the network began building more durable infrastructure: onboarding supports, clearer deliverables, workgroups, standards transition support, and shared statewide systems.

  • Buddy System emerged to support new PoCs entering the network
  • Statement of Work deliverables became organizing structures for the whole network
  • Datacasting and PA PBS work highlighted support for offline and rural learners
  • STEELS transition work began intensifying as Pennsylvania prepared for new science standards
  • Technology & Engineering immersive experiences pushed engineering beyond isolated activities
  • Workgroups became the network’s distributed implementation engine
What changed: The network moved from event and program coordination toward repeatable systems and shared capacity.
TRANSFORMATION 2023–2025

STEELS, AI, Ecosystems, and the Statewide Innovation Hub

The current phase is defined by convergence. STEELS implementation, AI literacy, environmental literacy, ecosystems, workforce partnerships, competitions, and inclusive STEM are no longer separate efforts. The PoC Network increasingly functions as Pennsylvania’s STEM implementation nervous system.

  • STEELS rollout became the center of statewide science and STEM support
  • AI literacy and responsible use emerged as urgent statewide needs
  • Environmental Literacy & Sustainability hubs connected STEM to place, climate, and community
  • Ecosystem and workforce partnerships expanded the network’s role beyond schools
  • ORBIT and SoW reporting became opportunities for impact storytelling, not just compliance
  • Workgroups generated statewide implementation intelligence across 13 active groups
What changed: The network transitioned from managing initiatives to shaping the future of STEM learning across Pennsylvania.
WHY THIS STORY MATTERS

It Preserves Memory

This network has years of learning that should not disappear when roles, grants, or leadership change.

It Accelerates Onboarding

New PoCs need more than a contact list. They need context, pathways, and a sense of how the work evolved.

It Reveals Patterns

Across years of agendas and minutes, the same needs keep resurfacing: implementation support, equity, translation, resources, and coherence.

It Builds Identity

The network is not just a meeting series. It is a statewide infrastructure for STEM collaboration, innovation, and impact.

Without this network, districts would solve the same problems in isolation.
With this network, Pennsylvania can learn in public, share faster, and build smarter together.

ORBIT REPORTING COMPANION

ORBIT is where STEMinPA work becomes documented evidence. Even if the tool changes next year, the thinking stays the same: name the work, connect it to a deliverable, explain the impact, and attach the proof. Move from activity tracking to impact storytelling.

A — ORBIT DECODER

Plain-language translation of the six things ORBIT asks you for in every entry.

Field 1
Activity / Event
What actually happened? Name the work — a workshop, a meeting, a convening, a resource you built.
Field 2
Deliverable Alignment
Which SoW bucket does this support? See the SoW Menu above — pick A through G.
Field 3
Description / Narrative
Why did this work matter? Two to four sentences connecting what happened to the bigger goal.
Field 4
Participants / Audience
Who was served or involved? Educators, administrators, students, community partners — be specific about numbers when possible.
Field 5
Evidence / Artifact
What proves the work happened? Agendas, sign-in sheets, resources, photos, links — attach or reference everything.
Field 6
Impact / Outcome
What changed, launched, improved, connected, or moved forward? Even early-stage outcomes count here.
B — WHAT DO I LOG THIS AS?

Select the type of work you did and get a suggested deliverable match with evidence and sample ORBIT language.

Why it fits

Collect this evidence

Sample ORBIT language

C — SAMPLE ORBIT LANGUAGE BUILDER

Use this template as a starting point for every ORBIT entry narrative. Fill in the brackets with your specifics.

During [month / timeframe], [IU / team] supported [audience] through [activity]. This work aligned to Deliverable [letter] by [connection to SoW]. Evidence includes [artifact / evidence]. Impact included [outcome / change / next step].

Example Entries

DEL B
STEELS Professional Learning

During October, IU staff supported district science educators through a STEELS professional learning session focused on three-dimensional instruction and classroom implementation. This work aligned to Deliverable B by supporting standards implementation across regional districts. Evidence includes the agenda, slide deck, participant list, and follow-up resources. Impact included increased teacher readiness and identified next steps for district-level implementation.

DEL C
AI / CS Professional Learning

During November, IU staff facilitated an AI and computer science professional learning experience for educators and administrators. This work aligned to Deliverable C by supporting CS integration, AI literacy, and computational thinking connections. Evidence includes the session agenda, shared resources, and participant feedback. Impact included increased educator confidence and new district conversations around responsible AI use.

DEL E
STEM Ecosystem Partner Meeting

During January, IU staff convened regional education, workforce, and community partners to identify opportunities for STEM ecosystem collaboration. This work aligned to Deliverable E by strengthening cross-sector partnerships and connecting classroom learning to regional workforce needs. Evidence includes meeting notes, partner attendance, and action steps. Impact included new collaboration opportunities and clearer next steps for regional STEM programming.

Think in Evidence, Not Just Events

Strong reporting is not just listing activities. It captures what the work actually accomplished:

  • Educator impact — who was reached, what shifted in practice
  • Student reach — direct or indirect contact with learners
  • Workforce engagement — partners, employers, industry connections
  • Systems change — district plans, policies, or structural shifts
  • Resource creation — tools, guides, or materials that persist after the event
  • Collaboration growth — new partnerships, cross-IU connections, network depth
  • Implementation progress — evidence that work led to sustained follow-through
Good evidence tells the story of why the work mattered.

// The Work Is the Network

The SOW is more than a compliance document — it is a statewide coordination framework. Every PoC working their deliverables is simultaneously building a shared professional infrastructure for Pennsylvania STEM: shared resources, shared learning, shared evidence, shared momentum.

When you log ORBIT entries, attend workgroup meetings, contribute to the STEM Toolkit, or show up to a regional PLC — you are not just doing your job. You are contributing to a network that would not exist without each of its nodes showing up.

The network itself is one of Pennsylvania's most important STEM resources.
