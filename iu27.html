Mission & Vision

Mission

BVIU's mission is to provide innovative educational programming, leadership, and resources for all learners. Their vision centers on shaping the future by facilitating learning opportunities and ensuring excellence in education, services, and support across Beaver County.

Regional Identity

  • Executive Director: Dr. Eric G. Rosendale
  • Serves 14 brick-and-mortar districts, 5 charter/cyber schools, 11 non-public schools in Beaver County
  • Primary direct-service identity: New Horizon School (ages 5–21, 230 students)
  • Lean SSoS staff (~2.5 FTE) creating both agility and capacity constraints
  • STEM identified as a high-interest, high-need area where BVIU can deliver with expertise
  • Community partnerships and local business engagement cited as an explicit organizational strength
STEM Signals & Technology Posture
STEELS

STEELS Delivery to All LEAs

BVIU provides STEELS standards training to all 14 brick-and-mortar districts, 5 charters, and the CTC — at least one session per year. Customized STEM sessions hosted at BVIU quarterly; 12 districts have requested custom sessions.

STEM High-Need Area

STEM as Strategic Lever

STEM is explicitly identified in the plan as a high-interest, high-need area in which BVIU can deliver with expertise — signaling organizational intent to grow STEM delivery even within a lean SSoS staffing model.

Transition / Career Readiness

Center-Based Transition Programming

Transition planning offered at grades 6–8 and 9–12 center-based levels with explicit career readiness and life skills components. Transition services from school to workplace listed as a core student service.

Behavior Services

Countywide Behavior Interventionists

Behavior interventionists deployed to support every brick-and-mortar school in the county except one. New Mental Health Support Services endeavor expanding with after-school community connections.

Assessment Technology

CDT / Firefly / IXL Ecosystem

CDTs increasingly utilized across 12 districts; Firefly referenced for online testing support. IXL used as a benchmark assessment platform 4x/year. SAS Portal and PASA DLM Website for standards training and curriculum alignment.

AI / Technology

No AI-Specific Initiatives

No AI tools, digital learning platforms, or technology integration programs are named in the 2025–2028 plan. Technology services focus on CDT/online testing support and web-based curriculum resources updated annually.

Named Initiatives & Programs
01

New Horizon School

Public special education center-based school serving 230 students with disabilities ages 5–21 across PreK through grade 12 bands. Career readiness and life skills embedded across all grade levels.

02

Structured Literacy & PA ELA Standards Initiative

Multi-year effort to adapt PA Core ELA standards for special education students using inclusive, structured literacy practices. Targeting 100% of SpEd teachers and a 25% proficiency increase by Year 3.

03

MTSS and ELA Resource Initiative

PD and resource integration effort targeting MTSS process alignment with PA standards for students with disabilities. Goal: 90% of SpEd teachers reporting increased confidence in using MTSS resources.

04

Mental Health Support Services

New endeavor providing school-based counseling and therapy with after-school supports via community connections — responding to growing student mental health complexity across Beaver County.

05

Early Intervention Expansion

Continued expansion of EI services to daycares and preschools to prevent suspension and expulsion of young children — a proactive investment in the earliest intervention pipeline.

06

STEM Sessions (Quarterly)

STEM sessions hosted at BVIU at least once per quarter. Customized STEM sessions delivered to member districts on request — 12 of 14 districts have accessed custom sessions during this planning period.

Strengths & Challenges

Regional Strengths

  • Strong community partnerships and local business engagement enabling new service development
  • Data-driven, needs-responsive approach to LEA service customization
  • Expanded behavior interventionist services reaching virtually every brick-and-mortar district school
  • STEM identified as a high-interest, high-need area where BVIU can deliver with expertise
  • Consistent service access: 14 brick-and-mortar and 5 charter/cyber schools regularly accessing services
  • Leadership driving culture change around high expectations with families and classrooms

Challenges & Gaps

  • Adapting PA Core Standards for students with significant and complex disabilities
  • Finding high-quality resources aligned to PA standards for students with extreme disabilities
  • Recruiting and retaining high-quality teachers without a dedicated HR department
  • Limited SSoS staff capacity (~2.5 FTE) to meet all State System of Support requirements
  • Fiscal sustainability as programs and services expand to meet LEA needs
  • Space limitations and transportation challenges as the IU grows
Plan Priorities
A

ELA Curriculum Alignment & Adaptations

By Year 3, adapt PA Core ELA standards for all students with IEPs, delivering targeted PD to 100% of SpEd teachers and achieving a 25% increase in proficiency among center-based students.

B

Structured Literacy Professional Learning

By Year 3, ensure 85% of educators in school-based systems and LEAs receive training on PA Core ELA adaptations, with a 20% increase in student engagement as the outcome measure.

C

ELA Resource Adoption

By Year 3, identify and integrate at least 10 high-quality PA-aligned resources for students with disabilities, with 95% of SpEd teachers reporting effective implementation.

D

MTSS with ELA Focus — Teacher Training

By Year 3, deliver MTSS process PD to 100% of SpEd teachers, with 90% reporting increased confidence in using MTSS resources for students with complex disabilities.
