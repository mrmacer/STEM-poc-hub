Mission

Luzerne IU18 supports member districts across Luzerne County by providing specialized programs, transition services, and professional learning designed to ensure equitable access to education and meaningful pathways to post-secondary success for all learners.

Regional Identity

  • Serves Luzerne County public schools
  • Headquarters: 368 Tioga Avenue, Kingston, PA
  • Emphasis on workforce development, community school models, and transition for students with disabilities
  • Named employer-connected programs for 11th–12th grade students
  • Early learning and family engagement as core service lines
STEM Signals & Technology Posture
Workforce

Luzerne Learns to Work

Employer-connected career readiness program for 11th–12th grade students, integrating LinkedIn Learning and Metrix Learning platforms with local employer partnerships to build workforce-ready credentials.

Transition Services

Comprehensive Transition Pathways

CBVT, CLTC, job shadowing, paid work experience, PA LAE, and itinerant transition specialists provide multiple coordinated pathways from school to post-secondary employment and independence.

Community Schools

PAL — Personalized Academy of Learning

Mentoring, literacy acceleration, and career pathway program serving students who need individualized alternative approaches. Community-school model integrating library and community resources.

Career Readiness

Guest Teacher Program

Fast-track pathway for bachelor's degree holders to serve as substitute teachers — addressing regional educator shortage while building a pipeline for long-term staff development.

AI / Technology

Digital Learning Platforms

LinkedIn Learning and Metrix Learning integrated into Luzerne Learns to Work. REAL Academy uses technology-mediated instruction. AI/CS strategy not explicitly detailed in extractable content.

Environmental / Stewardship

Dream Green Farm

Experiential learning program with agricultural and environmental education components, providing community-connected STEM-adjacent learning opportunities for participating students.

Named Initiatives & Programs
01

Luzerne Learns to Work

Employer-connected career readiness for grades 11–12 using LinkedIn Learning and Metrix Learning. Bridges classroom learning to local employer expectations.

02

Lighthouse Academy / Lighthouse Academy at Mehoopany

Alternative education program offering structured academic support and behavioral intervention in community-based settings across the county.

03

PAL — Personalized Academy of Learning

Mentoring, literacy, and career pathway program for students requiring personalized learning approaches. Integrates Luzerne County Library as community partner.

04

Dream Green Farm

Experiential agricultural and environmental learning program connecting students to stewardship, ecology, and career opportunities in land-based industries.

05

Document Destruction Depot (DDD)

Work-based learning enterprise providing vocational training and competitive integrated employment experience for students with disabilities.

06

REAL Academy

Technology-mediated alternative learning program offering flexible, personalized educational pathways for students with complex needs or credit recovery requirements.

07

Pineapple Projects

Project-based enrichment initiative fostering creative, hands-on learning and cross-curricular connections for participating students.

08

Guest Teacher Program

Fast-track pathway enabling qualified bachelor's degree holders to serve as substitute teachers, addressing regional staffing shortages and building a local educator pipeline.

09

SpEd Certification Fast-Track

Accelerated special education certification pathway for paraprofessionals and career changers, strengthening IU18's capacity to staff specialized programs with qualified educators.

Plan Priorities
A

Workforce & Career Readiness

Scaling Luzerne Learns to Work, expanding employer partnerships, and embedding LinkedIn Learning / Metrix Learning credentials into 11th–12th grade programming.

B

Transition Services Continuum

Maintaining a comprehensive, individualized transition architecture — CBVT, CLTC, paid work experience, itinerant specialists — to maximize post-secondary outcomes for students with disabilities.

C

Equity & Inclusive Access

Expanding UDL, community school partnerships, and alternative education programs to reach students with the greatest barriers to traditional educational success.

D

Educator Pipeline Development

Guest Teacher Program and SpEd Certification fast-track as regional solutions to educator shortages, building long-term staffing capacity from within the community.
