Mission & Vision

Mission

NEIU 19 is an educational agency that supports diverse learner needs by collaborating with families, educators, and community partners to deliver innovative leadership, programs, and services. Its vision is to be the regional leader in advancing education and community partnerships across northeastern Pennsylvania.

Regional Identity

  • Executive Director: Dr. Kathleen Sottile
  • Address: 1200 Line St, northeastern PA
  • Serves students ages 3–21 across member school districts and charter schools
  • Strong orientation toward special education programming (center-based and school-based)
  • Active student competition promotion as a named IU service
  • Steering committee includes Lackawanna College, ProABA, St. Joseph's Center
STEM Signals & Technology Posture
Student Competitions

Active Competition Partnerships

NEIU 19 explicitly promotes and runs student competitions for all students across its footprint, partnering with multiple agencies and organizations. Competition promotion is cited as a current strength and ongoing core service.

STEELS

PA STEELS Resources

PA STEELS standards are referenced as a curriculum resource provided to parents, signaling active integration with the state's new science standards framework. STEM services described as available to all schools in the IU footprint.

Transition Pathways

Multi-Stage Transition Services

Transition from school to work, transition from EI to school-age services, and transition from infant/toddler to preschool EI are all explicitly listed as student services. OVR connection support is offered to families.

Career Readiness

Career & Transition Opportunities

Career-readiness opportunities are listed as a community service commitment. Paired with OVR partnership, this signals emerging workforce-transition infrastructure.

AI / Technology

Technology Support for PD

Technology assistance cited as a required resource across multiple action plan steps. Technology support system purchase identified as a near-term need for modernizing PD delivery. No AI-specific platforms named in plan.

Professional Learning

Multi-Modal PD Expansion

NEIU is actively working to diversify PD delivery from single-model to multi-modal formats, including technology-assisted delivery, to increase access across member districts. Tracked via Frontline PD data.

Named Initiatives & Programs
01

Student Competitions Program

Active partnerships with agencies and organizations to promote and run a multitude of student competitions for districts across the NEIU 19 footprint. Explicitly cited as a standing core service and organizational strength.

02

PA Cycle of Continuous Improvement

Evidence-based framework guiding the expansion of professional development delivery models across all NEIU departments, with structured data monitoring via Frontline.

03

Recruitment Strategies Initiative

Structured effort to develop 2+ university partnerships for intern certificated candidate hiring within 3 years, directly addressing reliance on emergency-certified personnel as a staffing risk.

04

BCBA Partial Hospitalization Program

Behavioral support services for students with significant behavioral needs requiring structured therapeutic and educational intervention beyond the standard school day.

05

Right to Education Local Task Force #19

Community and parent engagement structure with monthly meetings and parent training, ensuring families of students with disabilities have structured access to IEP process guidance and advocacy support.

Strengths & Challenges

Regional Strengths

  • Active student competition partnerships — explicitly described as a standing strength
  • Evidence-based, standards-aligned curriculum and assessment facilitation
  • Effective instructional strategy implementation across programs
  • Opening new special education programs responsive to district needs
  • New outpatient therapy partial program launching (fall 2023)
  • Consistent tracking and reporting of SSOS/IDEA data — meets all requirements annually

Challenges & Gaps

  • Staffing shortages and reliance on emergency-certified personnel
  • Limited funds for curriculum and resource acquisition
  • Stakeholder engagement and communication — ongoing development area
  • Districts lack substitute coverage to access NEIU PD workshops
  • Single-model PD delivery limiting participant access
  • Possible capacity gaps if LEA needs assessment reveals demand beyond current staff capacity
Plan Priorities
A

Multi-Modal PD Delivery

Using PD Services data, NEIU 19 will utilize more than one delivery model for at least 6% of PD offerings by end of plan period to increase educator participation and access.

B

University Intern Pipeline

Develop 2+ formal partnerships with local universities to hire intern certificated candidates within 3 years, replacing emergency-certified staff with fully qualified educators.

C

Student Competition Expansion

Continue and expand partnerships with agencies and organizations to offer a multitude of student competitions for all districts in the NEIU 19 footprint as a core service commitment.

Cross-IU Collaboration Opportunities
🏆

Competitions Infrastructure

NEIU 19's explicit competition promotion model — with multi-organization partnerships — is a strong candidate for statewide Competitions workgroup alignment and cross-IU sharing.

📺

Multi-Modal PD Modernization

NEIU's structured effort to diversify PD delivery could benefit from shared learning with IUs further along in hybrid and online professional development models.

📚

University Intern Pipeline

The structured intern certificated candidate recruitment strategy is a replicable model for IUs facing similar emergency-certification dependencies — a statewide workforce challenge.

💼

Transition Program Development

NEIU provides transition services but lacks named work-based learning programs or industry credentials. Collaboration with IUs having established CTE articulation (OSHA10, ServSafe) could accelerate capacity.
