Regional Context

Regional Identity Snapshot

Midwestern IU 4 serves Butler, Lawrence, and Mercer Counties in western Pennsylvania, operating as a broad-spectrum educational services agency. MIU IV's service model spans special education, curriculum and instruction support, technology, workforce transition, pre-K programming, federal program consortium administration, homeless/foster care services, and community outreach. The dominant strategic posture in this plan is explicitly revenue-generating and outward-facing: the IU recognizes it has not effectively marketed its services and has made community engagement and brand awareness its headline priorities. MIU IV is notable as the statewide administrator of the largest federal programs Title I/II/IV-A consortium in Pennsylvania.

Signature Work

Named Initiatives & Programs

Active

Pennsylvania Pre-K Counts

Full-day pre-K for eligible 3–4 year olds in Mercer and Lawrence Counties; serves children at risk at up to 300% of federal poverty level; five classrooms via STAR 4 and Head Start partnerships.

Active

State Parent Advisory Council (SPAC)

MIU IV operates the statewide SPAC on behalf of PDE; provides Title I Parent and Family Engagement resources via spac.k12.pa.us; conducts annual statewide conference.

Active

LCT — Life-Centered Technology

Supports pregnant/parenting teens in obtaining employable skills and continuing education across Butler, Lawrence, and Mercer Counties via family center and church partnerships.

Active

TaC (Training & Consultation)

Technical assistance for districts: Autism, AT, LRE/Inclusive Practices, MTSS, PBS, Secondary Transition/Interagency, Special Ed Compliance, and PULS for principals.

Active

Guest Teacher Training Program

Trains bachelor's degree holders to become substitute teachers; issues Emergency Day-to-Day Substitute Certificates across all three counties.

Active

Homeless/Foster Care (Region 5)

Advocacy, outreach, McKinney-Vento and ESSA Foster Care compliance, student supplies, and referrals across Butler, Lawrence, and Mercer Counties.

Strengths + Challenges

Organizational Direction

Plan Priorities

01

Partnership Between MIU IV and Schools

By March 31, 2027, at least 90% of school systems in all three counties will have purchased at least 3 services, trainings, or programs from MIU IV.

02

Client Satisfaction

By March 31, 2027, at least 90% of teachers or administrators will report being "satisfied" or "highly satisfied" with any MIU IV service, training, or program.

03

Website & Social Media Growth

Website analytics will increase by 70% and social media analytics by 40% by March 31, 2027 (using March 2024 as baseline).

Cross-IU Collaboration Opportunities
