WG-ELS // WORKGROUP PROFILE

Environmental Literacy & Sustainability / Careers & EL&S

Building sustainable statewide EL&S systems — MWEE pathways, green careers, ELIT hubs, student ambassador programs, and equitable place-based learning experiences across Pennsylvania.

Leads: Sara Jeffries (DCIU) · Jen Sciacca (CAIU)
Cadence: 2nd Thursdays, 9am
Active / System-Building

What This Group Does

Focus: This workgroup moves beyond one-off environmental education into statewide systems — building MWEE pathways, connecting EL&S to green careers and workforce development, supporting ELIT hubs, and integrating environmental literacy with the ecosystems and sustainability lens across the PoC network.
WHY
Purpose & Intent
What we're here to do
Core ▾

Environmental literacy and sustainability work is scattered across IUs — every region has something, but there's no connective tissue. This workgroup builds that tissue: shared frameworks, shared resources, and shared strategy so place-based EL&S work adds up to something statewide.

The Careers & EL&S subgroup specifically works to connect students to green career pathways, integrate workforce development into environmental education, and make EL&S visible as an economic and equity issue — not just a science topic.

MWEE
MWEE Pathways
Meaningful Watershed Educational Experiences — beyond the 101
Active ▾

The MWEE 101 has been delivered widely, but the question now is: what comes next? This workgroup is building the pathway beyond 101 — deeper implementation, teacher-ready resources, and MWEE Ambassador Day toolkits that IUs can adapt regionally.

  • MWEE Ambassador Day toolkit development
  • Eco-Themed MWEE Ambassador Day model
  • Shared Waters and watershed connections
  • STEELS in Your Schoolyard integration
CRGR
Green Careers & Workforce
Connecting EL&S to student futures
Active ▾

The green careers lane connects environmental literacy directly to workforce development and student futures. The Green Careers Day template gives IUs a replicable event model; the Careers & EL&S subgroup is building the connective tissue between student experiences and employer/community pathways.

  • Green Careers Day template — replicable event model
  • Student ambassador programs and career exposure
  • MWEE connections to CTE and workforce pipelines
  • Regional employer and community partnership maps
ELIT
ELIT Hubs & Coordination
Environmental Literacy Integration Teams statewide
Infrastructure ▾

ELIT hubs are the organizational home for place-based environmental literacy work at the regional level. This workgroup supports their development and helps PoCs understand how ELIT connects to STEELS implementation, MWEE pathways, and statewide EL&S strategy.

  • ELIT hub coordination and capacity-building
  • Connecting IU PoCs to regional ELIT structures
  • Ecosystem and community partnership integration
