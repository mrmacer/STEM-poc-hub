Identity

Mission & Regional Context

Mission & Vision

Foster innovation in learning across a predominantly rural four-county region. Vision: inspire people to exceed their dreams through high expectations, professional growth, and community-connected education.

Regional Reality

All four counties are classified rural by the Center for Rural Pennsylvania. Population decline of 4–9% per county since 2010, lower-than-state bachelor's degree attainment, and higher poverty/disability rates define the strategic landscape.

STEM Signals

Named Initiatives & Workforce Programs

01

STEM Lending Network

Active service offering tracking district and business participation; metrics used as plan performance indicators. Primary PAIMS-relevant program in AIU8's portfolio.

  • District participation tracked as performance measure
  • Business involvement tracked alongside district reach
02

Regional Career Resource Guide

Planned deliverable compiling local career pathways and partner businesses, to be distributed to all 35 districts by June 2026. Led by Janel Vancas and Jennifer Anderson.

  • All 35 districts receive completed guide
  • Replicable template for rural IUs statewide
03

Industry Site Visits

Minimum 4 annually targeting at least 25 students and 10 educators per visit. Educator PD sessions focused on local industry integration — 4 annually.

  • 4+ business partnerships targeted by Dec 2025
  • Chambers of commerce and economic development orgs as entry points
04

18 Essential Practices Framework

AIU8's core school improvement framework driving diagnostic support and professional development across all 35 member districts. Diagnostic Team planned as embedded support initiative.

  • District-level performance reporting
  • 5+ annual PD workshops with business-partnered public events
Organizational Health

Strengths & Challenges

Strengths

  • PD infrastructure and networks across 35 school districts
  • Data analytics capacity with internal team
  • Existing relationships with chambers of commerce and economic development organizations
  • Capacity-building support for administrators as instructional leaders
  • Evidence-based instructional strategy support (operational)

Challenges

  • Creating shared community identity across dispersed four-county rural region
  • Business legitimacy — must be seen as credible partner by employers
  • Fiscal coordination across local, state, and federal resources
  • Human capital explicitly flagged as a barrier in needs assessment
  • Sustaining two-way stakeholder communication across region

Plan Priorities

2024–2027 SMART Goals

01

Best Instructional Practices Region-Wide

By June 2026, create data-driven improvement framework including district-level performance reporting, 5+ annual PD workshops, and business-partnered public events showcasing instructional achievements.

Industry-School Collaboration

By June 2026, establish 4+ business partnerships, facilitate 10+ industry site visits for students and educators, and distribute a regional career resource guide to all 35 districts.
