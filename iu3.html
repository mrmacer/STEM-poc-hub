Regional Context

Regional Identity Snapshot

The Allegheny Intermediate Unit is one of Pennsylvania's largest and most complex IUs, serving all 42 suburban school districts of Allegheny County, plus non-public, charter, and vocational-technical schools. The agency operates three schools for exceptional children, 10 family centers, and approximately 130 programs for children, adults, and families. AIU3 functions as a regional hub for professional development, school improvement support, equity services, data governance, STEM programming, and transition services — with reach documented across all 42 member districts. Its strategic posture in this plan cycle is internally focused: workforce pipeline and data-driven resource allocation are the two headline priorities.

Strengths + Challenges

Stated Strengths

  • All 42 member districts participate in STEM activities
  • Code.org and CS programming region-wide
  • CPE enrollment increased 36% year-over-year; 95%+ completion rates
  • Equity services reached 70% of member districts
  • 100+ days of networked learning community work annually
  • Brian Stamford: 90 days CDT PD/TA statewide as state lead

Challenges / Opportunity Radar

  • Staffing/Recruitment — #1 priority; tight labor market
  • Data-driven resource allocation with volume/complexity of data
  • Administrator engagement in equity work (very few participating)
