THE ORIGIN STORY

The PA STEM PoC Network didn't start as a formal structure — it grew from a shared problem: IUs across the commonwealth were doing great STEM work in isolation. The PAsmart grant ecosystem created the infrastructure to change that. Today, this network is one of the most active cross-IU collaboration spaces in Pennsylvania education.

Your role as a PoC (Point of Contact) is to be the STEM connective tissue for your IU — linking PDE priorities, statewide resources, and your local districts into something coherent and useful.

The Org Triangle

PAIMS (ed tech + instructional media) · PAIUCC (curriculum coordinators) · STEM PoC (you) — these three groups share an annual joint meeting every March and increasingly overlap in mission.

FOUNDATION

PAsmart Launches STEMinPA

Federal grant funding creates the framework for statewide IU collaboration around STEM, CS, and student-centered learning.

GROWTH

Workgroups Proliferate

Science, T&E, Environmental Literacy, CS, Student Competitions, Equity/TaC/Inclusion — the network self-organizes around real practitioner needs.

NOW

Full Ecosystem Integration

STEM PoCs are embedded in ORBIT data reporting, WestEd CoPs, STEELS rollout, and the emerging AI/CS frontier. You're joining at a pivotal moment.

DEL A STEM PoC Network

Participation in the statewide PoC network — meetings, workgroups, collaboration.

PoC Meetings Workgroups Statewide PLCs
This is your baseline deliverable. Showing up to PoC meetings, joining workgroups, and contributing to shared resources all count here. Your TaC also plays a role in supporting your workgroup participation.

DEL B STEELS Standards

Supporting PA Science/STEM standards implementation across your IU and districts.

Science T&E EL&S MWEE PENNSEL
STEELS is the big one. Most of the statewide professional learning (WestEd CoPs, PaTTAN institutes) connects here. Your TaC often co-facilitates STEELS PD. The Equity/TaC/Inclusion workgroup is building UDL-integrated resources to make STEELS accessible for ALL learners.

DEL C Computer Science Education

CS integration, CSTA standards, AI/ML literacy, computational thinking.

CS Workgroup AI Taskforce Comp Thinking
CS + AI is the fastest-moving deliverable right now. New CSTA standards are shifting toward AI/ML process understanding (not just tool use). IUs doing tiered AI PD models, CT cohorts, and district AI workshops all log here.

ORBIT Sample: [PLACEHOLDER]
DEL D Student-Centered Learning

Advancing SCL approaches in STEM contexts across districts.

STEM Pals PASCL CoP StartSOLE
SCL overlaps heavily with PASCL CoP work. STEM Pals is the think-tank workgroup for collaboratively building shared PD materials. StartSOLE is a student-driven inquiry model that fits here.

DEL E STEM Ecosystems

Building and sustaining regional STEM ecosystems through partnerships.

PSSE Workgroup Ecosystem Convenings
Ecosystems work is now housed in the PSSE Workgroup (led by Abbey Hathaway, IU12). This involves connecting schools, businesses, community orgs, and higher ed into functioning regional STEM ecosystems.

DEL F Student Competitions

Coordinating and supporting regional and statewide STEM competitions.

Keystone STEM Sea Air & Land Invention Convention Esports
This is one of the most tangible deliverables for districts — they can see and feel it. Jillian Darlington leads the statewide Student Competitions Workgroup. Competitions Kick-Off is typically late October.

DEL G Coherent Integrative STEM / COE

Resources for accelerated learning, math integration, UDL + STEM connections.

STEM Toolkit Equity/TaC Lending Library Math Focus
G is the catch-all for integrative work — UDL + STEM, math + STEM, equity + STEM. The Equity/TaC/Inclusion workgroup has been doing deep work here this year, including drafting STEELS guides for Special Education teachers.
