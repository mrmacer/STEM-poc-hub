PAIMS / STEM PoC Network Story · 2018–Present

The story of a statewide network learning out loud.

The PA STEM PoC Network grew from grant coordination, computer science planning, and regional STEM support into a statewide implementation infrastructure for STEELS, AI literacy, ecosystems, competitions, environmental literacy, workforce pathways, and shared professional learning.

From meetings → to workgroups → to statewide STEM intelligence.
This is not just the story of a meeting series. It is the story of how Pennsylvania’s Intermediate Units built a shared learning infrastructure for STEM implementation. Across years of agendas, workgroups, initiatives, and statewide conversations, the network moved from isolated regional support toward a more connected system for solving problems, sharing resources, and building collective capacity.

2018 –2019 Foundation

The network begins naming its shared work

PAsmart grants created the first statewide coordination infrastructure. IUs began meeting regularly around STEM, computer science, and student-centered learning. Carnegie STEM Pathway helped districts assess readiness. SCRIPT spread K–12 CS planning across the state. The network didn’t yet have a name for what it was building — but it was beginning.

  • PAsmart grants funded IU coordination around STEM and CS
  • Carnegie STEM Pathway introduced structured district readiness conversations
  • SCRIPT (Strategies for Creating Inclusive and Progressive Technology) spread statewide
  • STEM Toolkit conversations began shifting from activities to vetted instructional resources
  • IUs started functioning as statewide connectors, not just regional service providers
PAsmart SCRIPT Carnegie CS STEM Toolkit
2020 Pandemic Response Resilience

Pandemic turns collaboration into survival infrastructure

The pandemic forced an emergency pivot. PoC meetings became something more than coordination — they became a statewide support system. Every IU was solving the same problems simultaneously. The network’s collaborative instincts turned out to be its most important asset.

  • Human-first check-ins became structural, not occasional
  • Remote STEM support and virtual delivery became immediate operational challenges
  • Lending libraries adapted to new access and sanitization logistics
  • Equity and access moved from aspirational to operational
  • The Statement of Work began crystallizing informal support into defined deliverables
Pandemic Remote Learning Equity Lending Library SoW
2021 Recovery Phase Recovery

Professional learning gets rebuilt in public

With schools re-opening, the network rebuilt professional learning in real time. Not just returning to old formats, but rethinking what STEM PD could be in a changed landscape. Lessons from remote delivery shaped in-person practice.

  • Hybrid and in-person PD models rebuilt with new insights from remote delivery
  • Datacasting and PA PBS work highlighted support for offline and rural learners
  • Inclusive practice work deepened — MTSS, UDL, and accessibility in STEM
  • IUs shared tactics, templates, and lessons learned across regions in real time
  • The buddy system concept began taking shape as onboarding support emerged as a need
PD Hybrid Inclusion PBS MTSS UDL
2021 –2022 · Open Spaces Culture

Open Spaces become the engine room

Open Space sessions emerged as one of the network’s most generative formats. Instead of structured agendas, participants named their own working conversations. The network discovered it could self-organize around real practitioner needs — not just pre-planned topics.

  • Open Space sessions surfaced topics the network hadn’t formally named before
  • Practitioner-generated conversations replaced top-down agendas in key meetings
  • Persistent themes began organizing naturally into proto-workgroups
  • Topics emerged organically: lending libraries, competitions, math identity, ecosystems
  • The format revealed that the network held more distributed knowledge than any agenda could capture
Open Space Self-Organizing Culture Workgroups
2022 Workgroup Formation Structure

The group shifts from meetings to work streams

Workgroups emerged as the network’s distributed engine. Rather than discussing everything in whole-group meetings, the network moved real work into focused groups organized around specific themes and deliverables. Cross-IU collaboration became the norm.

  • Workgroups proliferated: Science/STEELS, T&E, EL&S, CS, Student Competitions, Equity/TaC/Inclusion
  • The Statement of Work’s deliverable structure began aligning with workgroup themes
  • Cross-IU collaboration became the norm, not the exception
  • Workgroup leads gained clearer roles and responsibilities
  • The ORBIT data system began capturing this distributed implementation work
Workgroups SoW Deliverables ORBIT Cross-IU
2023 Standards Era STEELS

Standards, ecosystems, and student-centered learning converge

STEELS, Pennsylvania’s new science standards framework, became the organizing center. But the network saw more convergence: environmental literacy, ecosystem partnerships, student competitions, and workforce pathways were all pointing toward the same vision of student-centered STEM learning.

  • STEELS rollout became the center of statewide science and STEM support work
  • Ecosystem and workforce partnerships began connecting STEM to community and industry
  • Environmental Literacy & Sustainability emerged as a major cross-cutting thread
  • Student competitions, Esports, and project-based learning connected STEM to real engagement
  • MWEE (Meaningful Watershed Educational Experiences) integrated outdoor and place-based learning
STEELS Ecosystems MWEE Competitions EL&S Workforce
2024 Network Reset Leadership

The reset: purpose, structure, goals, and shared leadership

The network took time to revisit its foundations. A period of intentional reset — clarifying purpose, refining structure, strengthening shared leadership, and preparing for the next phase of growth and sustainability.

  • Network leadership conversations deepened across IUs
  • Purpose and shared identity were re-examined and articulated
  • The Statement of Work evolved to better reflect where the network was heading
  • New PoC onboarding became a priority — the network needed better pathways for incoming members
  • Statewide intelligence and documentation work began in earnest
Leadership Reset SoW Onboarding Identity
2024 –2025 · AI Emergence AI

AI becomes the newest test of the network’s purpose

Generative AI arrived as both a challenge and an opportunity. The network faced the same question it always faces when something new arrives: how do we help districts and IUs navigate this responsibly, equitably, and effectively?

  • AI literacy and responsible use emerged as urgent statewide needs
  • Multiple IUs launched AI professional development programs for educators and administrators
  • Questions of equity, access, and bias shaped the network’s approach to AI adoption
  • AI connected to ongoing CS, data science, and computational thinking work
  • The network’s human-first orientation became its guide in navigating AI’s arrival
AI CS Data Science Equity PD Literacy
2025 Intelligence Layer Intelligence

From archive to operating system

Years of meeting notes, agendas, workgroup outputs, and institutional knowledge pointed toward a new possibility: what if the network’s accumulated intelligence could be made accessible, searchable, and useful in real time? The gap between what the network knew and what new PoCs could access became visible and urgent.

  • The idea of a “living knowledge system” began taking shape
  • STEM Intelligence Layer work connected workgroup activity to pattern recognition
  • The gap between what the network knew and what new PoCs could access became clearly visible
  • Documentation and onboarding became a shared infrastructure priority
  • The hub project was born from this question: how do we make the network’s knowledge available to itself?
Intelligence Documentation Onboarding Knowledge Memory
Now Current Build Hub Build

The PA STEM PoC Hub becomes the network’s living interface

The hub brings onboarding, workgroups, directory intelligence, SoW alignment, ORBIT reporting support, IU profiles, project portfolios, and workgroup pages into one shared statewide interface. The network can now move from static documentation toward searchable statewide STEM intelligence.

  • New PoCs can understand the network faster through the 90-day onboarding pathway
  • Returning PoCs can find people, projects, and resources more easily via the mission-node directory
  • Workgroups can preserve action items, artifacts, and institutional memory through profile pages
  • IU profiles show regional strengths, initiatives, and collaboration opportunities
  • The network can begin moving from static documentation toward searchable statewide STEM intelligence
