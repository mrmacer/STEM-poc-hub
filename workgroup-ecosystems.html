WHY
Purpose & Intent
What we're here to do — and what this space is for
Core ▾

This is a peer support network for ecosystem builders — not a delivery vehicle for statewide initiatives. The vibe is think tank, not task force. Any ecosystem is welcome, whether you've been at it for a decade or just inherited the role from someone who left.

  • Ecosystems seeking support from other ecosystems — share ideas, collaborate, commiserate
  • Space to talk through the management and growth challenges that don't have textbook answers
  • Help each IU build a clearer vision of where their ecosystem is headed
  • Break down what PSSE statewide priorities actually mean at the regional level
  • Build collective skill at convening disparate groups — what models exist? What communication tools work?

Deliverables are mostly TBD intentionally — this is a space for shared resources, shared ideas, and collecting real stories of ecosystem growth oriented around the problem and how it's still being solved (Paul Smith framework).

Mini-Grant Design
Building ecosystem mini-grant frameworks · STEMinPA funding
Active Discussion ▾

A major thread since January 2026 — how to structure mini-grants that actually build the ecosystem rather than just fund IU-adjacent projects.

Design principles emerging from the group:
  • Keep applications short — long applications are prohibitive and kill participation
  • Require partnership between at least 2 organizations (list sector examples to spark thinking)
  • Align to ecosystem priorities — must include cross-sector partnership requirements
  • Think beyond K-12: OST providers, higher ed, industry partners are the point — ecosystem perspective, not IU perspective
  • Specify clearly: can funds go to individuals or organizations only?
  • Be prepared to distribute funds differently by district or partner type
Stipend vs. reimbursement debate:
  • Reimbursement = teacher/district fronts money, then submits receipts — more admin burden
  • Stipend = issued as requirements are completed, with MOU and accountability measures
  • Remake Learning model: no receipt requirement, accountability = event is held, called "awards" not grants
  • W-9s required regardless; 1099-NEC limit increasing to $2000 for 2026
Open questions:
  • Does all money have to be spent before end of fiscal year?
  • Tax question — who pays? How do we keep this from being a barrier to participation?
  • Could a Remake Learning event serve as an accountability measure?
  • OST partners (Boys & Girls Clubs, libraries) are hurting but have the deepest community reach — how do we include them?
BACK
Backcasting as a Facilitation Tool
Rapid future-visioning sprint · Ecosystem convening methodology
Tool / Method ▾

Surfaced at the October STEM Ecosystem Convening via Steph Schwab. A Rapid Backcasting Sprint sets a vision for an ideal state in 2050, then works backward — "what would need to be done in 2040, 2030, 2025 to get there?" — broken down across sectors: policy, innovation, social capital, resources.

  • Structured conversation with timed discussion for each sector
  • Encourages thinking past barriers rather than getting hung up on them
  • Steph recommends an outside facilitator
  • AI can generate scenario frameworks tailored to your ecosystem's needs and goals
  • Scenarios can be revised to align with PA statewide goals

The group discussed running this at a PSSE Convening (Jan or June) with a facilitation debrief so IUs can bring it back to their regions. Longer-term goal: build a toolkit of community-building activities for ecosystem leaders, starting with Backcasting.

STEMinPA Grant Project Ideas
What IUs are funding with their ecosystem grant dollars
Reference ▾

From the September 2025 kickoff — IUs sharing what they're using STEMinPA grant funds for:

  • Berks (Kendy): Industry + internship coordinator + STEM teacher panel events; steering committee supplies & food; rebranding Career Ready Berks. Career Ready sites are a huge lift — is there a role for PSSE to step in?
  • FrAY / IU12 (Abbey): CTE panel event; DC Convening expenses
  • Mini-grants (Jen, IU15 model): Budget + proposal → district buys supplies → submits receipts → reimbursement follows. Used for EL&S — required lesson plan aligned to STEELS + ELIT PD attendance + receipts + action photos as accountability
  • BCIU: AI and careers-focused panel event
  • IU5 + IU4: Partnering with Penn West — AI in industry event
  • DCIU: AI panel with student-generated questions; leveraged ecosystem for panelists

Pattern emerging: ecosystem engagements (panels, competitions, judges) are creating an industry pipeline that benefits other IU events too.
